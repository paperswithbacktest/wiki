## What is the Papua New Guinea Kina?

The Papua New Guinea Kina is the official money used in Papua New Guinea. It is called 'Kina' and it is divided into smaller units called 'Toea'. One Kina is equal to 100 Toea. People in Papua New Guinea use Kina to buy things like food, clothes, and other goods.

The Kina was first used in 1975 when Papua New Guinea became its own country. Before that, they used the Australian Dollar. The Kina is important because it helps the country control its own money and economy. You can see the Kina in different amounts, like 2, 5, 10, 20, 50, and 100 Kina notes, and also in coins.

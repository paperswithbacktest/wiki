## What is options trading and how does it work on Robinhood?

Options trading is a way to buy or sell a stock at a certain price before a certain date. It's like making a bet on whether a stock's price will go up or down. When you buy an option, you're not buying the stock itself, but the right to buy or sell it later. There are two types of options: calls and puts. A call option gives you the right to buy a stock, while a put option gives you the right to sell it.

On Robinhood, options trading is made simple and accessible. To start, you need to get approved for options trading by filling out a short questionnaire about your investing experience and risk tolerance. Once approved, you can trade options right from the Robinhood app. You can search for a stock, then look at the options available for that stock. You'll see different expiration dates and strike prices. When you find an option you like, you can buy it with just a few taps. Robinhood also provides tools and educational resources to help you understand options better and make informed decisions.

## What advanced options strategies can I use on Robinhood?

On Robinhood, you can use advanced options strategies like covered calls and protective puts. A covered call is when you own a stock and sell a call option on that stock. This can give you extra income from the option's premium, but it also means you might have to sell your stock if the price goes up a lot. It's a way to make some money while still holding onto your stock, but it limits how much you can gain if the stock's price goes way up. A protective put is when you own a stock and buy a put option on that stock. This can protect you if the stock's price goes down because you can sell it at the price you agreed on in the put option. It's like insurance for your stock, but it costs money to buy the put option.

Another advanced strategy you can use on Robinhood is a straddle. A straddle is when you buy both a call option and a put option on the same stock with the same expiration date and strike price. This strategy is good if you think the stock's price will move a lot but you're not sure which way. If the stock's price goes up a lot, you can make money from the call option. If it goes down a lot, you can make money from the put option. But if the stock's price doesn't move much, you could lose money because you paid for both options.

## What are the tax implications of options trading on Robinhood?

When you trade options on Robinhood, you need to think about taxes. The money you make from options trading is usually seen as capital gains by the IRS. If you hold an option for less than a year before selling it, any profit you make is considered a short-term capital gain. Short-term gains are taxed at the same rate as your regular income, which can be pretty high depending on how much you make. If you hold an option for more than a year, any profit is a long-term capital gain, which is taxed at a lower rate, usually between 0% and 20%.

It's also important to know that you might have to pay taxes on the money you make from options even if you don't sell the stock. If you exercise an option and then sell the stock in the same year, the profit from the stock sale is added to the profit from the option, and that's what you're taxed on. Keeping good records of all your trades is really important because you'll need to report all your gains and losses on your tax return. Robinhood gives you a tax document called a Form 1099-B that shows your trading activity, but it's up to you to figure out your taxes and report them correctly.

## What is Preferred Equity Redemption Stock (PERCS)?

Preferred Equity Redemption Stock (PERCS) is a type of preferred stock that comes with a set end date. This means that after a certain period, usually a few years, the stock will automatically convert into a set number of shares of the company's common stock. PERCS is designed to give investors a higher dividend than regular common stock, but it also has a limit on how much the investor can gain if the stock price goes up a lot.

The main attraction of PERCS for investors is the promise of regular dividends and the potential for some growth in the stock's value. However, there's a catch: if the stock price goes above a certain level by the end date, the investor only gets the set number of common shares, not more. This can be a good choice for someone who wants a steady income and is okay with limited growth potential, but it might not be the best fit for someone looking for big gains from rising stock prices.

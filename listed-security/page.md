## What is a listed security?

A listed security is a financial product, like a stock or bond, that is officially traded on a stock exchange. When a company wants its stocks or bonds to be bought and sold by the public, it applies to a stock exchange to have them listed. Once approved, these securities can be easily traded by investors on that exchange.

Listing a security on an exchange helps a company gain more visibility and credibility. It also makes it easier for the company to raise money because more people can invest in it. For investors, listed securities are generally seen as safer because they are regulated by the exchange and must follow strict rules.

## How does a security become listed on a stock exchange?

To get a security listed on a stock exchange, a company first has to meet certain rules set by the exchange. These rules can include things like how much money the company makes, how many shares it wants to sell, and how much the company is worth. The company also has to fill out a lot of paperwork and pay fees. Once everything is ready, the company sends its application to the stock exchange.

After the stock exchange gets the application, it checks everything to make sure the company meets all the rules. This can take some time. If everything is okay, the exchange will approve the listing. Then, the company's security can start trading on the exchange. This means people can buy and sell the company's stocks or bonds easily. If the application is not approved, the company has to fix any problems and try again.

## What are the benefits of listing a security on a stock exchange?

Listing a security on a stock exchange helps a company in many ways. It makes the company more well-known and trusted because being on a stock exchange shows that the company follows strict rules. This can attract more investors who want to buy the company's stocks or bonds. Also, it's easier for the company to raise money because more people can invest in it. When a company needs money to grow or start new projects, it can sell more shares on the exchange.

For investors, listed securities are often seen as safer. This is because the stock exchange keeps a close watch on the companies to make sure they follow the rules. Investors can also easily buy and sell these securities during trading hours, which makes it more convenient. Plus, being on a stock exchange can help the company's stock price go up, which is good news for people who own the stock.

## What are the different types of listed securities?

Listed securities come in different types, like stocks and bonds. Stocks are pieces of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you own a small part of that company. Bonds are like loans that you give to a company or government. They promise to pay you back with interest over time. Both stocks and bonds can be listed on a stock exchange, which means they can be easily bought and sold by investors.

There are also other types of listed securities, such as exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and real estate investment trusts (REITs). ETFs are like baskets of different investments, like stocks or bonds, that you can buy and sell on an exchange. REITs are companies that own and manage real estate properties, and they can be listed on a stock exchange too. All these different types of securities give investors many choices to put their money into, depending on what they want to achieve with their investments.

## How does listing affect the liquidity of a security?

When a company lists its security on a stock exchange, it makes the security more liquid. Liquidity means how easy it is to buy or sell something without affecting its price too much. When a security is listed, more people can see it and trade it. This means there are usually more buyers and sellers, so it's easier to find someone to trade with. This can help keep the price stable because there's always someone ready to buy or sell.

Listing on a stock exchange also helps because the exchange has rules and systems that make trading smooth. For example, the exchange keeps track of all the trades and makes sure everything is fair. This makes investors feel more confident about trading the security. So, when a security is listed, it becomes easier to turn it into cash quickly, which is what [liquidity](/wiki/liquidity-risk-premium) is all about.

## What are the ongoing obligations of a company with listed securities?

Once a company's securities are listed on a stock exchange, it has to follow certain rules to stay listed. This means the company has to keep sharing information with the exchange and the public. They need to tell everyone about important things like how much money they are making, any big changes in the company, or anything else that might affect the price of their securities. This is called financial reporting, and it helps investors make good choices.

The company also has to follow the rules set by the exchange all the time. This includes having enough shareholders and making sure their stock price stays above a certain level. If the company doesn't follow these rules, the exchange might take away their listing. This can be bad for the company because it makes it harder for them to raise money and can make investors lose trust in them. So, it's really important for the company to keep up with these obligations.

## What are the risks associated with investing in listed securities?

Investing in listed securities can be risky. One big risk is that the price of the security can go up and down a lot. This means you could lose money if you need to sell your stocks or bonds when their price is low. Another risk is that the company might not do well. If the company has problems, like not making enough money or facing a lawsuit, the price of its securities can drop. This can hurt your investment.

There are also risks from things happening in the world that you can't control. For example, a big economic crisis or a natural disaster can affect the whole stock market, making all securities lose value. Sometimes, the information a company shares might not be completely true or might be hard to understand. This can make it tough to know if you're making a good investment. So, it's important to think about these risks before you invest in listed securities.

## How can investors analyze the performance of listed securities?

Investors can analyze the performance of listed securities by looking at different numbers and reports. One important thing to check is the company's financial statements, like their income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement. These show how much money the company is making, how much they owe, and how they are spending their cash. Investors also look at ratios like the price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio, which compares the stock's price to how much money the company makes. A lower P/E ratio might mean the stock is a good deal, but it's important to compare it with other companies in the same industry.

Another way to analyze listed securities is by keeping an eye on the stock's price over time. Investors can use charts and graphs to see how the price has changed and look for patterns. They might also read news and reports about the company to understand what's happening that could affect the stock's price. For example, if a company announces a new product or a big deal, the stock price might go up. By putting all this information together, investors can make smarter choices about buying or selling the securities.

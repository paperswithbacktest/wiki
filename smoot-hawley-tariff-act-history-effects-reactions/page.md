## What is the Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act?

The Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act was a law passed by the United States in 1930. It raised taxes on many imported goods to protect American businesses. The idea was to make foreign products more expensive, so people would buy things made in the U.S. instead. This was during the Great Depression, a time when many people were out of work and the economy was struggling.

However, the act ended up making things worse. Other countries got angry and put high taxes on American products in return. This made it harder for American businesses to sell their goods abroad. As a result, trade between countries decreased, and the global economy suffered even more. Many people now see the Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act as a mistake that made the Great Depression last longer.

## What is the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT)?

The Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) is a place where people buy and sell things like corn, wheat, and soybeans. It started a long time ago in 1848. Farmers and businesses come here to agree on prices for their crops before they are even harvested. This helps them know how much money they will make and helps keep food prices stable.

The CBOT is also famous for trading futures and options. Futures are contracts where people agree to buy or sell something at a certain price in the future. Options give people the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell at a certain price. These tools help people manage risk and plan for the future. The CBOT is now part of a bigger company called the CME Group, which runs other trading places too.

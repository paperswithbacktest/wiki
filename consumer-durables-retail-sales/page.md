## What are consumer durables?

Consumer durables are products that last a long time and are used by people over many years. These items are not used up quickly like food or soap. Instead, they are meant to be durable, meaning they can withstand wear and tear. Examples of consumer durables include appliances like refrigerators, washing machines, and televisions, as well as furniture and cars.

People usually spend a lot of money on consumer durables because they are big purchases. They often need to plan and save up for these items. Because they last a long time, consumer durables can affect a household's budget for many years. When these products break down or become outdated, people might need to repair them or buy new ones, which can be costly.

## How do consumer durables differ from non-durables?

Consumer durables and non-durables are different because of how long they last and how people use them. Durables are things that last a long time, like a couch or a laptop. They are meant to be used over many years and can handle being used a lot. When you buy a durable, you usually spend more money because it's a big purchase that you expect to keep for a while.

Non-durables, on the other hand, are things that don't last long. They get used up quickly, like food, soap, or paper towels. People buy these items more often because they need to replace them all the time. Non-durables are usually cheaper than durables because they don't last as long and you have to keep buying them.

The main difference between the two is their lifespan and how often you need to buy them. Durables are a big investment that you use for a long time, while non-durables are everyday items that you use up and replace regularly.

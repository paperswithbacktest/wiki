## What are floaters and fixed income bonds?

Floaters, or floating rate bonds, are a type of bond where the interest rate changes over time. Instead of having a fixed interest rate like regular bonds, the interest rate on floaters goes up or down based on a benchmark, like the LIBOR or the federal funds rate. This means that if the benchmark rate goes up, the interest you get from the floater will also go up, and if the benchmark rate goes down, your interest will go down too. This can be good for investors who want to protect themselves from rising interest rates.

Fixed income bonds, on the other hand, are bonds that have a fixed interest rate throughout their entire term. When you buy a fixed income bond, you know exactly how much interest you will earn each year until the bond matures. This makes them more predictable and less risky than floaters because the interest payments stay the same no matter what happens to interest rates in the economy. Fixed income bonds are popular among investors who want a steady and reliable source of income.

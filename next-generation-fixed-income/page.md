## What is fixed income?

Fixed income refers to investments that provide a steady and predictable stream of income, usually in the form of interest payments. These investments are often in the shape of bonds, which are loans made to a government or corporation that promise to pay back the borrowed money with interest over a set period. The term "fixed" comes from the fact that the interest rate, or the coupon, is typically set at the time of issuance and does not change.

People like fixed income investments because they can offer a more stable and predictable income compared to stocks, which can be more volatile. Fixed income investments can be a good choice for people who want to protect their money and earn a steady return, like retirees who need regular income to live on. However, the returns from fixed income investments are usually lower than what you might get from stocks, so they are often used as part of a balanced investment strategy.

## Why is fixed income important in investment portfolios?

Fixed income is important in investment portfolios because it helps balance risk and reward. When you invest in stocks, the value can go up and down a lot, which can be scary. Fixed income investments, like bonds, don't change in value as much. They give you a steady income, which can make your whole investment plan more stable. This is good if you want to protect your money and not lose sleep over big swings in the market.

Another reason fixed income is important is that it can provide regular income. If you're retired or just need money coming in regularly, fixed income investments can help. They pay you interest at set times, so you know when the money is coming. This can be really helpful for planning your finances. Plus, having some fixed income in your portfolio can help you stay calm during times when the stock market is doing badly, because you know you still have that steady income coming in.

## What are the traditional types of fixed income securities?

Traditional fixed income securities include government bonds, corporate bonds, and municipal bonds. Government bonds are loans that you give to a government, like the U.S. Treasury bonds. They are usually seen as very safe because the government is less likely to go bankrupt. Corporate bonds are loans to companies. They can be riskier than government bonds but often offer higher interest rates to make up for that risk. Municipal bonds are loans to local governments, like cities or states. They can be used to fund things like schools or roads and sometimes have tax benefits.

Another type of fixed income security is the certificate of deposit (CD). CDs are offered by banks and they work by you giving the bank money for a set period of time, and in return, the bank pays you interest. CDs are usually very safe because they are insured by the government up to a certain amount. Lastly, there are also preferred stocks, which are a bit different because they are a type of stock but act more like a bond. They pay fixed dividends and are less risky than regular stocks but can still offer higher returns than traditional bonds.

## How does the next generation of fixed income differ from traditional fixed income?

The next generation of fixed income, often called alternative fixed income, is different from traditional fixed income because it tries to do more than just give you a steady income. It also aims to grow your money a bit more and protect it from big market swings. These new types of investments might include things like private debt, where you loan money to private companies, or asset-backed securities, where your loan is backed by things like car loans or credit card debt. They can also include investments in real estate or even infrastructure projects. These new options can be riskier than traditional bonds but might offer the chance for higher returns.

Another big difference is how these new fixed income investments are managed. Instead of just buying and holding bonds until they mature, managers of next-generation fixed income might use more active strategies. They might trade more often, use computer models to pick investments, or even use complex financial tools to try to make more money or reduce risk. This means these investments can be more flexible and might adapt better to changing market conditions. But because they're more complex and sometimes riskier, they might not be suitable for everyone and could need more watching and understanding than traditional fixed income investments.

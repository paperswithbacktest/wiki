## What is the Mozambique New Metical?

The Mozambique New Metical is the official currency of Mozambique, a country in Southeast Africa. It replaced the old Metical in 2006 to help control inflation and make the economy more stable. The symbol for the New Metical is MT, and its ISO code is MZN. People in Mozambique use this currency every day to buy things like food, clothes, and other goods.

The New Metical is divided into 100 centavos, but because of inflation, coins smaller than 1 metical are not used much anymore. Banknotes come in different amounts, like 20, 50, 100, 200, 500, and 1000 meticais. The value of the New Metical can change compared to other currencies, like the US dollar or the Euro. This can affect how much things cost for people in Mozambique and how much money the country makes from things it sells to other countries.

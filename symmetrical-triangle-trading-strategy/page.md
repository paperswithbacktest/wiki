## What is a symmetrical triangle pattern in trading?

A symmetrical triangle pattern in trading is a chart pattern that traders use to predict where the price of a stock or other asset might go next. It looks like a triangle on the chart, with two lines coming together to form a point. One line goes up, showing the highs of the price, and the other line goes down, showing the lows of the price. As these lines get closer, it means the price is moving into a smaller and smaller range, which can mean that a big move is coming soon.

When traders see a symmetrical triangle, they often think that the price will break out of the triangle soon. This breakout can happen in either direction, up or down. Traders watch the price closely as it gets near the point of the triangle. If the price breaks out above the top line, it might be a good time to buy. If it breaks out below the bottom line, it might be a good time to sell. The key is to wait for the price to actually break out before making a decision, because the direction of the breakout can be hard to predict.

These two lines should get closer and closer to each other until they meet at a point, which is why it's called a symmetrical triangle.

Once you see this shape, you can confirm it's a symmetrical triangle if the price keeps moving between these two lines, getting squeezed into a smaller and smaller range. The [volume](/wiki/volume-trading-strategy) of trades usually gets lower as the triangle forms, which means fewer people are buying and selling.

## What is the Commodity Exchange Act (CEA)?

The Commodity Exchange Act (CEA) is a law in the United States that was created to regulate the trading of commodities. Commodities are things like wheat, oil, and gold. The CEA helps make sure that trading these items is fair and that people don't cheat. It was first passed in 1936 and has been updated many times since then to keep up with changes in the market.

The main job of the CEA is to prevent fraud and manipulation in commodity markets. It does this by setting rules that people and businesses must follow when they trade commodities. The Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) is the government agency that makes sure these rules are followed. The CEA also helps protect people who invest in commodities by making sure they have clear information about what they are buying and selling.

## What are the key amendments made to the CEA over the years?

Over the years, the Commodity Exchange Act (CEA) has been changed many times to keep up with new ways of trading and to make the market safer. Another important change came in 1982 with the Futures Trading Act, which made it easier for people to trade futures contracts and also set up rules to stop people from cheating in the market.

More changes came in 2000 with the Commodity Futures Modernization Act (CFMA). This law made it easier for people to trade new kinds of financial products, like swaps, which are agreements to trade money based on things like interest rates or stock prices.

## What is a naked put option?

A naked put option is when someone sells a put option without owning the underlying stock. It's called "naked" because the seller doesn't have any protection if the stock price goes down. When you sell a put option, you're promising to buy the stock at a certain price, called the strike price, if the person who bought the option decides to use it. If the stock price drops below the strike price, the seller could lose a lot of money because they have to buy the stock at a higher price than it's worth in the market.

Selling naked puts can be risky, but it can also be a way to make money if the stock price stays above the strike price. The seller gets to keep the money they received for selling the option, which is called the premium. If the stock price stays above the strike price until the option expires, the option becomes worthless, and the seller keeps the premium as profit. However, if the stock price falls below the strike price, the seller might have to buy the stock at a loss. Because of this risk, naked puts are considered an advanced trading strategy and are not recommended for beginners.

## What is the impact of time decay on naked put options?

Time decay is a big deal when you sell naked put options. It's the idea that the value of an option goes down as it gets closer to the day it expires. When you sell a naked put, you want the option to become worthless so you can keep the money you got for selling it. Time decay helps you because every day that goes by without the stock price falling below the strike price, the option loses some value. This means the person who bought the option is less likely to use it, and you're more likely to keep the money you got.

The closer the option gets to expiring, the faster it loses value. This can be good for you if you're selling naked puts because it means the option is less likely to be used against you. But it's important to remember that time decay works both ways. If the stock price is close to the strike price and there's still a lot of time left, the option can still be worth a lot, and the buyer might use it. So, you need to think about how much time is left until the option expires when you decide to sell a naked put.

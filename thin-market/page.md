## What is a thin market?

A thin market is when there are not many people buying or selling something. This can happen with stocks, bonds, or other things you can trade. When a market is thin, it can be hard to find someone to buy or sell with. This can make prices move a lot because even a small trade can change the price.

In a thin market, it can be risky to trade. If you want to sell something, you might have to lower your price a lot to find a buyer. If you want to buy, you might have to pay more than you expected. This is because there are fewer people to trade with, so the few trades that happen can have a big impact on the price.

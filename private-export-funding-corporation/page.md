## What is the Private Export Funding Corporation (PEFCO)?

The Private Export Funding Corporation, or PEFCO, is a company that helps American businesses sell their products to other countries. It does this by providing loans to foreign buyers so they can purchase American goods. PEFCO was created in 1970 and is owned by a group of big banks in the United States. The main goal of PEFCO is to support American exports by making it easier for foreign customers to buy American products.

PEFCO works closely with the U.S. government, specifically with the Export-Import Bank of the United States (EXIM Bank). The EXIM Bank guarantees the loans that PEFCO gives to foreign buyers, which means it promises to pay back the loans if the buyers cannot. This makes it safer for PEFCO to lend money. By doing this, PEFCO helps American companies grow their business overseas and supports the U.S. economy.

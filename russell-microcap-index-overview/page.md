## What is the Russell Microcap Index?

The Russell Microcap Index is a stock market index that tracks the performance of the smallest companies in the U.S. stock market. It includes about 1,000 of the smallest companies that are part of the Russell 3000 Index, which represents the 3,000 largest publicly traded companies in the U.S.

This index is important because it gives investors a way to see how these very small companies are doing. These companies are often newer or less well-known, and they can grow a lot if they do well. By looking at the Russell Microcap Index, investors can decide if they want to invest in these small companies or not.

These tiny companies, called microcaps, are usually the smallest 1,000 companies in the bigger Russell 3000 Index.

By having this index, people can see if these small companies are growing or not. This can help investors decide if they want to put their money into these small companies, which might grow a lot if they do well.

## How often is the Russell Microcap Index updated or rebalanced?

The Russell Microcap Index gets updated and rebalanced once a year. This happens in June. The reason for doing this is to make sure the index still shows the smallest companies in the U.S. stock market correctly.

During the rebalance, the index checks which companies are the smallest 1,000 in the Russell 3000 Index. If some companies have grown bigger, they might be taken out of the Russell Microcap Index. And if new, very small companies have come into the Russell 3000 Index, they might be added to the Russell Microcap Index. This way, the index stays up to date with the tiniest companies.

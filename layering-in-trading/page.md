## What is layering in trading?

Layering in trading is a type of illegal practice where someone tries to trick other traders. They do this by placing many orders to buy or sell a stock, but they don't really want to complete these trades. The goal is to make the stock's price move in a way that benefits the person doing the layering. For example, they might put in lots of orders to buy a stock at higher prices to make it look like there's a lot of demand. This can push the price up, and then they can sell their own shares at this higher price.

Once the price has moved the way they wanted, the person doing the layering cancels all those fake orders. This leaves other traders confused and possibly losing money because they thought the market was different than it really was. Layering is considered a form of market manipulation and is against the rules set by financial regulators. It's important for traders to be aware of such practices to protect themselves and maintain a fair trading environment.

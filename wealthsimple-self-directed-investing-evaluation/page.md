## What is Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing?

Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing is a way for people to invest their money in the stock market on their own. It's like having a toolbox where you can pick and choose which stocks or exchange-traded funds (ETFs) you want to buy. You don't need a lot of money to start, and you can do everything online through the Wealthsimple website or app. It's great for people who want to be in control of their investments and make their own decisions.

With Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing, you pay a small fee every time you buy or sell stocks or ETFs. This fee is called a commission, and it's usually less than what you might pay at a traditional bank or brokerage. The platform is easy to use, even if you're new to investing. You can see all your investments in one place, track how they're doing, and make changes whenever you want. It's a good choice for anyone who wants to learn about investing and grow their money over time.

## How does Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing differ from Wealthsimple's managed investing options?

Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing lets you pick and choose your own stocks and ETFs. You're in charge of everything, from deciding what to buy and sell to when to do it. You pay a small fee each time you make a trade, but there are no ongoing management fees. This option is great if you want to learn about investing and make your own decisions. It's like being your own boss in the world of investing.

On the other hand, Wealthsimple's managed investing options, like Wealthsimple Basic and Wealthsimple Black, take care of everything for you. You tell them how much risk you're okay with, and they build a portfolio of ETFs that matches your goals. They handle all the buying and selling, and you pay a yearly fee based on how much money you have invested. This is perfect if you don't want to spend time managing your investments and would rather let experts do it for you. It's like having a personal investment manager without the high costs.

## What are the fees associated with Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing?

With Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing, you pay a small fee every time you buy or sell stocks or ETFs. This fee is called a commission, and it's $0 for buying or selling ETFs and $0 for buying stocks. If you sell stocks, the fee is $4.99 per trade. There are no ongoing management fees, so you only pay when you make a trade. This makes it affordable if you don't trade very often.

There are no account fees or minimum balance requirements, so you can start investing with any amount of money. If you want to trade options, there's a fee of $0 per contract. Also, if you want to move your money out of Wealthsimple, there might be a fee for transferring your account to another broker, but this is not a regular fee and only happens if you decide to leave Wealthsimple.

## Are there any tax advantages or considerations when using Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing?

When you use Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing, you can take advantage of tax-sheltered accounts like TFSAs (Tax-Free Savings Accounts) and RRSPs (Registered Retirement Savings Plans). With a TFSA, any money you make from your investments, like dividends or capital gains, is tax-free. This means you don't have to pay taxes on the money you earn, which can help your investments grow faster. An RRSP lets you save for retirement by reducing your taxable income now, but you'll pay taxes when you take the money out later. Both types of accounts can help you save on taxes and make your investments more efficient.

There are also some tax considerations to keep in mind. If you're trading stocks outside of a TFSA or RRSP, you'll need to pay taxes on any profits you make. This includes capital gains, which are taxed at half your income tax rate, and dividends, which can be taxed at different rates depending on the type of dividend. It's a good idea to keep track of your trades and any income you earn so you can report it correctly on your taxes. Wealthsimple provides tools and resources to help you understand these tax rules, but it's always smart to talk to a tax professional if you have specific questions or a complicated situation.

## What are some common strategies for maximizing returns using Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing?

One common strategy for maximizing returns with Wealthsimple Self-Directed Investing is to diversify your portfolio. This means spreading your money across different types of investments, like stocks, ETFs, and even cryptocurrencies. By doing this, you reduce the risk of losing money if one investment doesn't do well. For example, you might invest in a mix of tech stocks, healthcare ETFs, and some Bitcoin. This way, if the tech sector has a bad year, your other investments might still do well and help balance things out.

Another strategy is to focus on long-term growth rather than trying to time the market. Instead of buying and selling stocks quickly to make a quick profit, you can invest in solid companies or ETFs that you believe will grow over time. This approach can be less stressful and often leads to better returns in the long run. For instance, you might choose to invest in an S&P 500 ETF, which tracks the performance of 500 large U.S. companies, and hold it for many years. This way, you benefit from the overall growth of the market without having to worry about short-term ups and downs.

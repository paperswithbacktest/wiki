## What are the largest banks in Canada?

The largest banks in Canada are often called the "Big Five." These banks are Royal Bank of Canada (RBC), Toronto-Dominion Bank (TD), Bank of Nova Scotia (Scotiabank), Bank of Montreal (BMO), and Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce (CIBC). RBC is the biggest one. It has a lot of money and many customers. TD is also very big and has many branches in the United States too.

These banks offer many services like checking and savings accounts, loans, and credit cards. They also help businesses with their money needs. Scotiabank is known for its work in other countries, especially in Latin America. BMO and CIBC are also important because they help many people and businesses in Canada.

These five banks are very important for Canada's economy. They help keep money safe and help people and businesses grow. People trust these banks because they have been around for a long time and are very stable.

## How is the size of a bank typically measured in Canada?

In Canada, the size of a bank is usually measured by looking at how much money it has and how many customers it serves. The most common way to measure a bank's size is by its total assets. Total assets include all the money the bank has, like cash, loans it has given out, and investments it has made. A bank with more total assets is considered bigger. For example, if Bank A has $1 trillion in assets and Bank B has $500 billion, Bank A is bigger.

Another way to measure a bank's size is by the number of customers it has and the number of branches it operates. A bank with more customers and more branches can be seen as larger because it serves more people and has a bigger presence in the community. For instance, if Bank C has 10 million customers and 1,000 branches, while Bank D has 5 million customers and 500 branches, Bank C would be considered larger in terms of customer base and physical presence.

## What is Tezos?

Tezos is an advanced blockchain network associated with its native digital token, known as Tez or tezzie (XTZ). As a multifunctional blockchain like other contemporary cryptocurrencies, Tezos supports a wide range of decentralized finance (DeFi) activities, applications, and non-fungible tokens (NFTs). One of Tezos' defining features is its approach to network evolution, which sets it apart from many blockchain alternatives. Traditional blockchains often require hard forks—a split into two separate chains—to implement significant updates or protocol changes. Such forks can lead to community divisions and technical complications. Tezos avoids these issues by utilizing an on-chain governance system, enabling the network to seamlessly integrate changes and updates without the need for disruptive forks.

Additionally, Tezos employs a Proof-of-Stake (PoS) consensus mechanism, distinguishing it from energy-intensive Proof-of-Work systems like that of Bitcoin. In Proof-of-Stake, validators are chosen based on the number of tokens they hold and are willing to "stake" as collateral, which proves to be a more energy-efficient and scalable solution for network consensus. This energy-efficient design makes it an appealing option for environmentally conscious blockchain users and developers seeking sustainable technology solutions.

Overall, Tezos’ innovative governance model and sustainable consensus mechanism underscore its position as a modern blockchain platform capable of evolving and adapting to new technological developments without the pitfalls commonly associated with traditional blockchain upgrades.

## History of Tezos

The conceptual foundation for Tezos was laid by Arthur Breitman in 2014, driven by the vision of creating a blockchain with superior governance capabilities. Arthur's background in mathematics and computer science fueled his interest in cryptocurrencies and decentralized systems. Recognizing the limitations of existing blockchain technologies, he sought to develop a new platform that could address these issues. He collaborated with his wife, Kathleen Breitman, who brought her own expertise in strategy and operations to the project. Together, they aimed to create a blockchain that could evolve seamlessly without the disruptive hard forks that characterized other blockchain systems.

Tezos gained considerable attention after its Initial Coin Offering (ICO) in July 2017. This ICO became one of the largest in history at the time, raising an unprecedented $232 million in just 13 days. The success of the ICO highlighted the promising potential and widespread interest in Tezos' innovative approach to blockchain adaptation and governance.

Despite the successful fundraising, Tezos faced significant challenges following its ICO. The project was marred by internal disputes involving the Tezos Foundation, which was established to oversee the development and deployment of the Tezos protocol. These disputes were primarily legal and operational in nature, stemming from disagreements between the Breitmans and the then-president of the Tezos Foundation, Johann Gevers. The conflict resulted in delays in the release and distribution of Tezos tokens (XTZ) and led to several lawsuits filed by investors.

Ultimately, however, these challenges were resolved, and the Tezos platform managed to navigate through the turmoil. Changes in the foundation’s governance and structure were implemented to facilitate smoother operations and align more closely with the Breitmans' vision. Consequently, Tezos successfully launched its mainnet in September 2018, allowing users to finally engage with the network and utilize its unique on-chain governance features. This resilience and ability to overcome early setbacks have been pivotal in establishing Tezos' position within the [cryptocurrency](/wiki/cryptocurrency) and blockchain spaces.

## Understanding Tezos Technology

Tezos operates as a decentralized ledger similar to widely recognized platforms such as Bitcoin and Ethereum. However, its distinctive feature is its self-amending protocol, enabling the blockchain to evolve and upgrade without the need for disruptive hard forks. This innovation addresses a significant challenge in blockchain technology: the ability to adapt and improve over time while maintaining consensus and network integrity.

The core of Tezos' technology lies in its governance model, which allows stakeholders to actively participate in the protocol's development. Stakeholders, or "bakers," as they are termed in the Tezos ecosystem, can propose amendments to the protocol. These proposals are subjected to a structured and transparent voting process. If a proposal gains sufficient support, it is implemented as a network upgrade. This mechanism fosters a democratic approach to blockchain evolution, allowing continuous adaptation in response to technological advancements and community needs.

The reliance on community governance rather than a centralized authority provides Tezos with a robust structure for future enhancements. This decentralized approach ensures that no single entity can dictate the direction of the network, thereby promoting security, transparency, and inclusivity. Additionally, by engaging a broad base of participants in its governance, Tezos creates an environment where diverse perspectives and expertise can contribute to the platform's growth and refinement.

Moreover, the self-amending feature and community-driven governance align with the broader principles of decentralized finance, where trustlessness and autonomy are paramount. These attributes not only differentiate Tezos from other blockchain platforms but also position it as a resilient and adaptable solution capable of navigating the dynamic landscape of digital finance.

## How Tezos Differs from Other Blockchain Platforms

Tezos sets itself apart from other blockchain platforms through its distinct formal governance protocol, which is designed to prevent forks and thus promote long-term stability. Unlike many traditional blockchains that lack a structured governance model, Tezos incorporates an on-chain mechanism allowing stakeholders to actively participate in decision-making. This not only mitigates the risk of divergent chains due to contentious updates but also streamlines the integration of improvements. 

A key aspect of Tezos' differentiation lies in its adoption of the Proof-of-Stake (PoS) consensus mechanism, which confers significant energy efficiency compared to the energy-intensive Proof-of-Work (PoW) protocols like that of Bitcoin. Under PoS, validators (referred to as "bakers" in the Tezos ecosystem) are selected based on the number of tokens held and "staked" as collateral, reducing the computational power required to validate transactions and secure the network. This positions Tezos as a more sustainable choice in the blockchain landscape.

Security is further enhanced by Tezos’ use of the Michelson smart contract language, which supports formal verification. Formal verification is a methodical approach to proving the correctness of algorithms underlying a smart contract. This mathematical technique minimizes execution risks and ensures the reliability of smart contracts, thereby reducing vulnerabilities to exploits and bugs that can lead to significant financial loss. By facilitating precise contract executions, Michelson boosts developer confidence and promotes the deployment of secure decentralized applications. 

Overall, Tezos’ emphasis on structured governance, energy efficiency, and robust security measures through Michelson smart contracts makes it a distinctive and attractive platform for developers and investors alike.

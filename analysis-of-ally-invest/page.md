## What is Ally Invest and what services do they offer?

Ally Invest is a company that helps people invest their money. It used to be called TradeKing before it was bought by Ally Financial. Ally Invest lets people buy and sell stocks, bonds, and other investments easily using their computer or phone. They have low fees, which means it doesn't cost a lot of money to use their services.

Ally Invest offers two main types of accounts: a self-directed account and a robo-advisor account. With a self-directed account, people can choose their own investments. They can trade stocks, options, ETFs, mutual funds, and more. The robo-advisor account is for people who want help with their investments. The robo-advisor uses computers to pick investments that match what the person wants, like saving for retirement or buying a house. Both types of accounts are easy to use and can help people grow their money.

## How does Ally Invest cater to beginners in investing?

Ally Invest makes it easy for beginners to start investing. They have a simple website and app that are easy to use. When you sign up, you can start with a self-directed account where you pick your own investments. Ally Invest has lots of tools and information to help you learn. They have videos, articles, and a practice account where you can try trading without using real money. This helps you get comfortable with investing before you start using your own money.

For beginners who want more help, Ally Invest also offers a robo-advisor called Managed Portfolios. With this, you answer some questions about your goals and how much risk you want to take. Then, the robo-advisor picks investments for you. It's like having a helper that does the work for you. This is great for people who are new to investing and want to start without having to learn everything right away. Ally Invest's low fees also make it easier for beginners to start investing without spending too much money.

## What are the fee structures associated with Ally Invest accounts?

Ally Invest has low fees which makes it easier for people to invest without spending too much money. For self-directed accounts, there are no fees to open an account. You can trade stocks and ETFs for free, which means you don't pay anything when you buy or sell them. Options trades cost $0.50 per contract. If you want to trade mutual funds, there is a $9.95 fee per trade for no-load funds. There are also no account maintenance fees or inactivity fees, so you don't have to worry about extra costs if you're not trading often.

For the robo-advisor service called Managed Portfolios, there is a fee based on how much money you have invested. If you have less than $25,000, the fee is 0.30% of your total account balance each year. If you have more than $25,000, the fee goes down to 0.25% per year. This fee is taken out of your account automatically, so you don't have to do anything. There are no additional trading fees with the robo-advisor because it manages your investments for you.

Ally Invest's trading platform is designed to be easy to use, even if you're new to investing. You can use it on your computer or on your phone with their app. When you log in, you see a clear screen that shows your investments and how they're doing. You can easily buy and sell stocks, options, ETFs, and mutual funds. There's a search tool that helps you find what you want to invest in quickly. You can also set up alerts to tell you when a stock reaches a certain price, which helps you keep track of your investments without having to watch them all the time.

The platform has lots of tools to help you make smart investment choices. There are charts and graphs that show how different investments have done over time. You can use these to see if a stock or [ETF](/wiki/etf-trading-strategies) might be a good choice for you. Ally Invest also has a tool called "Watchlists" where you can keep track of stocks you're interested in but haven't bought yet. Another helpful feature is the "Paper Trading" account, where you can practice buying and selling without using real money. This is great for learning how to invest without risking your money. Overall, the platform is user-friendly and has everything you need to start investing and keep learning.

Ally Invest offers a variety of investment products that you can choose from. You can trade stocks, which are shares in companies. You can also trade ETFs, which are like baskets of stocks that follow a certain market or industry. If you want a simpler way to invest, Ally Invest has a robo-advisor called Managed Portfolios. This service picks investments for you based on your goals and how much risk you're comfortable with. The robo-advisor mainly uses ETFs to build your portfolio. This can be a good choice if you don't want to pick your own investments but still want to grow your money. Overall, whether you want to pick your own investments or let a robo-advisor do it for you, Ally Invest has a range of products to help you invest in different ways.

## What are the security measures in place at Ally Invest to protect investor assets and data?

Ally Invest takes security very seriously to keep your money and information safe. They use something called two-[factor](/wiki/factor-investing) authentication, which means you need two ways to prove it's really you when you log in. This makes it harder for someone else to get into your account. They also use strong encryption to protect your data when it's sent over the internet. This is like putting your information in a special code that only Ally Invest can understand. Plus, they have systems that watch for anything unusual, like if someone tries to log in from a strange place or make a big withdrawal.

Another way Ally Invest keeps your money safe is by following rules from the government. They are part of the Securities Investor Protection Corporation (SIPC), which means if Ally Invest ever has big problems, the SIPC can help protect your investments up to $500,000, including $250,000 in cash. Ally Invest also has extra insurance from a company called Lloyd's of London, which adds more protection on top of what the SIPC offers. This extra insurance can cover up to $50 million, which gives you even more peace of mind that your money is safe.

## How does Ally Invest's robo-advisor service work and what are its benefits?

Ally Invest's robo-advisor service, called Managed Portfolios, is a way to invest your money without having to pick your own investments. When you sign up, you answer some questions about your goals and how much risk you're okay with. Based on your answers, the robo-advisor picks a mix of ETFs that fit what you want. It then keeps an eye on your investments and makes changes if needed to keep your portfolio on track. You don't have to do anything because the robo-advisor does all the work for you.

The benefits of using Ally Invest's robo-advisor are that it's easy and helps you grow your money without much effort. It's good for people who don't want to spend a lot of time learning about investing or making their own choices. The robo-advisor also has low fees, starting at 0.30% of your account balance per year, which means you keep more of your money. Plus, it's backed by Ally Invest's security measures, so you can feel safe about your investments.

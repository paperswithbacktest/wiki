## What is the Garn–St. Germain Depository Institutions Act?

The Garn–St. Germain Depository Institutions Act is a law that was passed in the United States in 1982. It was created to help banks and other financial institutions during a time when many of them were struggling. The main goal of the act was to make it easier for these institutions to offer different types of loans and financial services. This was done by reducing some of the rules and restrictions that were in place at the time.

One important part of the act was that it allowed banks to offer adjustable-rate mortgages (ARMs). These are home loans where the interest rate can change over time. This was a big change because before the act, most home loans had fixed interest rates. The act also made it easier for savings and loan associations to merge or take over other struggling institutions. While the act helped some banks in the short term, it also led to problems later on, as some banks took too many risks and eventually failed.

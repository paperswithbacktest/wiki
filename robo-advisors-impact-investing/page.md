## What are robo-advisors and how do they work?

Robo-advisors are automated platforms that provide financial advice and investment management services with minimal human intervention. They use computer algorithms to create and manage a diversified portfolio of investments based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and other personal information you provide. This makes investing easier and more accessible for people who might not have the time or knowledge to manage their investments themselves.

When you sign up with a robo-advisor, you'll usually start by answering a questionnaire about your financial situation and goals. The robo-advisor then uses this information to build a personalized investment portfolio, often using low-cost exchange-traded funds (ETFs). The platform automatically adjusts your investments over time to keep them aligned with your goals, and some robo-advisors also offer features like tax-loss harvesting to help you save money on taxes. Overall, robo-advisors make investing simpler and more affordable, though they may not offer the same level of personalized advice as a human financial advisor.

## What is impact investing and why is it important?

Impact investing is when you put your money into companies, organizations, or funds that aim to make a positive difference in the world, while also trying to make a financial return. These investments can focus on things like helping the environment, improving people's health, or supporting education. The idea is to use your money to help solve big problems and make the world a better place, not just to make more money.

Impact investing is important because it can bring more money to projects and causes that need it. By investing in these areas, you can help speed up solutions to big issues like climate change or poverty. Plus, it shows that you can care about making a difference and still think about your financial future. This way of investing encourages more people and businesses to think about how they can help society and the planet, not just their wallets.

## What are the potential drawbacks or limitations of using robo-advisors for impact investing?

One potential drawback of using robo-advisors for impact investing is that they might not offer as much personalized advice as a human financial advisor. While robo-advisors use computer programs to pick investments that match your goals, they can't always understand your unique situation or give you the detailed advice you might need. If you have complex financial needs or want to talk through your options with someone, a robo-advisor might not be the best fit.

Another limitation is that the range of impact investment options might be smaller with robo-advisors compared to working directly with a human advisor. Robo-advisors often use funds that focus on making a positive impact, but these might not cover all the areas you care about. If you want to invest in very specific causes or projects, you might find it hard to do that through a robo-advisor. So, while robo-advisors make impact investing easy, they might not give you all the choices you want.

## How do fees and costs compare between traditional robo-advisors and those focused on impact investing?

The fees and costs for traditional robo-advisors and those focused on impact investing can be pretty similar. Both types of robo-advisors usually charge a management fee, which is a small percentage of the money you have invested with them. This fee is often around 0.25% to 0.50% per year. The main difference might be in the funds they use. Impact investing robo-advisors might use funds that focus on making a positive impact, and these funds can sometimes have slightly higher fees than regular funds. But the difference is usually not very big.

When you look at the total cost, you need to think about the fees for the robo-advisor and the fees for the funds they invest in. For both traditional and impact investing robo-advisors, the total cost can be around 0.30% to 0.80% per year. This includes the robo-advisor's management fee and the fees for the funds. So, while impact investing robo-advisors might have a tiny bit higher costs because of the funds they use, the overall difference in fees between the two is usually small enough that it won't make a huge impact on your investment returns.

## How can an investor evaluate the impact of their investments when using a robo-advisor?

An investor can evaluate the impact of their investments by looking at the reports and updates provided by the robo-advisor. Most robo-advisors that focus on impact investing will give you regular updates on how your investments are doing and what kind of difference they are making. Another way to evaluate the impact is by checking the specific funds your robo-advisor has chosen for you. By understanding what your money is going towards, you can better judge if it's making the kind of impact you want.

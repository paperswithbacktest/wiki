## What is Obamanomics?

Obamanomics refers to the economic policies and initiatives implemented during Barack Obama's presidency from 2009 to 2017. These policies aimed to address the severe economic downturn caused by the 2008 financial crisis. Key aspects of Obamanomics included the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act, which was a large stimulus package designed to boost economic growth through government spending and tax cuts. Additionally, the Affordable Care Act, often called Obamacare, sought to reform healthcare by increasing access and reducing costs for millions of Americans.

Another important part of Obamanomics was financial regulation, specifically the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act. This law was created to prevent another financial crisis by regulating banks and financial institutions more strictly. Obama's administration also focused on education, investing in programs to improve schools and increase access to higher education. While Obamanomics had many supporters who believed it helped stabilize and grow the economy, it also faced criticism from those who felt it increased government spending and debt too much.

## Who coined the term 'Obamanomics'?

The term 'Obamanomics' was first used by a journalist named James Pethokoukis. He wrote about it in an article for the U.S. News & World Report in November 2008, just after Barack Obama was elected president.

Pethokoukis used the term to describe the economic policies that Obama was expected to implement. It became a popular way to talk about Obama's approach to fixing the economy during his time as president.

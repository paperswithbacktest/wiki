## What is a swing option?

A swing option is a type of financial contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy a certain amount of a commodity, like natural gas or electricity, over a period of time. The special thing about swing options is that the buyer can change, or "swing," the amount they buy each day within set limits. This flexibility helps the buyer manage their needs better, especially when the demand for the commodity can change a lot.

For example, a power company might use a swing option to buy natural gas. They can increase their purchase on cold days when more gas is needed to heat homes, and decrease it on warmer days. This helps them save money and avoid wasting resources. The seller of the swing option gets paid for providing this flexibility, but they also take on more risk because they don't know exactly how much the buyer will want each day.

Another way to price swing options is by using a model called the Stochastic Dynamic Programming (SDP) method.

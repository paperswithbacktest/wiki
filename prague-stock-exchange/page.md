## What is the Prague Stock Exchange?

The Prague Stock Exchange, also known as the Burza cenných papírů Praha (BCPP), is the main stock exchange in the Czech Republic. It is located in Prague and was established in 1992 after the country's transition from a communist economy to a market economy. The exchange plays a crucial role in the Czech financial market by providing a platform for trading stocks, bonds, and other securities.

The Prague Stock Exchange operates several indices, with the most well-known being the PX Index, which tracks the performance of the 13 most liquid stocks on the exchange. This index serves as a benchmark for the overall performance of the Czech stock market. The exchange also offers trading in derivatives and structured products, helping investors manage risk and diversify their portfolios. Overall, the Prague Stock Exchange is essential for the growth and development of the Czech economy, attracting both domestic and international investors.

## What types of securities are traded on the Prague Stock Exchange?

The Prague Stock Exchange lets people trade different types of securities. These include stocks, which are shares in companies, and bonds, which are like loans to companies or the government. Stocks and bonds are the most common securities you'll find on the exchange. People buy and sell these to invest their money and hopefully make a profit.

Besides stocks and bonds, the Prague Stock Exchange also trades other financial products. These include derivatives, which are contracts that get their value from something else, like a stock or a commodity. Another type is structured products, which combine different investments to offer something new. These help investors manage risk and try different investment strategies.

## What are the main indices of the Prague Stock Exchange?

The main index of the Prague Stock Exchange is the PX Index. This index tracks the performance of the 13 most liquid stocks on the exchange. It's like a scorecard that shows how well the biggest and most traded companies are doing. Investors use the PX Index to see how the overall market is performing and to compare their own investments.

Another important index is the PX-TR, which stands for PX Total Return. This index is similar to the PX Index but also includes dividends that companies pay to their shareholders. So, it gives a fuller picture of the returns you might get from investing in the stocks on the Prague Stock Exchange. Both indices help investors understand the market and make smart choices about where to put their money.

## Who regulates the Prague Stock Exchange?

The Prague Stock Exchange is regulated by the Czech National Bank (CNB). The CNB is like a big boss that makes sure the stock exchange follows the rules. They check that everything is fair and safe for people who want to invest their money. The CNB also makes sure that the companies on the stock exchange tell the truth about their business.

The CNB has the power to make new rules and change old ones to keep the stock exchange working well. They watch the market every day to stop any bad behavior. If someone breaks the rules, the CNB can punish them. This helps keep the Prague Stock Exchange a good place for people to invest their money.

## What are the trading hours of the Prague Stock Exchange?

The Prague Stock Exchange is open for trading from Monday to Friday. The main trading hours are from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM Central European Time (CET). This is when most of the buying and selling happens.

There are also pre-market and after-hours trading sessions. The pre-market session starts at 8:30 AM and goes until the main session begins at 9:00 AM. The after-hours session runs from 4:00 PM to 5:00 PM. These extra sessions let people trade outside the main hours, but not as many trades happen then.

## How has the Prague Stock Exchange performed historically?

The Prague Stock Exchange has had its ups and downs over the years. It started in 1992, right after the Czech Republic changed from a communist system to a market economy. At first, it grew quickly as people were excited about investing in new companies. The PX Index, which shows how the top stocks are doing, went up a lot in the early years. But then, like many stock markets, it faced some big drops. For example, during the global financial crisis in 2008, the PX Index fell a lot because people were worried about their money.

Since then, the Prague Stock Exchange has had some good times and some tough times. After the 2008 crisis, it slowly started to recover. By the mid-2010s, the PX Index was doing better again, and more people were interested in investing. But then, in 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic caused another big drop in the market. However, the exchange bounced back pretty quickly, showing that it can handle tough situations. Overall, the Prague Stock Exchange has been a key part of the Czech economy, helping it grow and change over the years.

## What are the listing requirements for companies on the Prague Stock Exchange?

To list on the Prague Stock Exchange, a company needs to meet certain rules. They must have a certain amount of money, called equity, which is usually at least 20 million Czech Koruna. The company also needs to have been in business for at least three years and show that it made money in the last year. They need to have a good number of shareholders, usually at least 100, and these shareholders must own at least 25% of the company's shares. The company also has to follow the rules about how it shares information with the public, making sure it's honest and clear.

Another important part of listing on the Prague Stock Exchange is the company's paperwork. They need to give the exchange a lot of documents, like their financial reports and a plan for how they will use the money they raise from selling shares. The company also has to agree to follow the exchange's rules and let the exchange check their business to make sure everything is okay. If a company can meet all these requirements, they can list their shares on the Prague Stock Exchange and start trading.

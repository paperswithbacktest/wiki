## What is the Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA)?

The Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) is the government authority responsible for maintaining monetary and banking stability in Hong Kong. It was established in 1993 and works to ensure that the Hong Kong dollar remains strong and trusted by people around the world. The HKMA manages the city's monetary policy, which includes controlling the amount of money in circulation and setting interest rates.

In addition to managing the currency, the HKMA also oversees and regulates banks and other financial institutions in Hong Kong. This means they make sure that banks follow the rules and operate safely, protecting customers' money. The HKMA plays a crucial role in keeping Hong Kong's financial system stable and reliable, which is important for the city's economy and its position as a major financial center in the world.

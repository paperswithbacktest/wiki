## Background of Ubiquant

Ubiquant Investment (Beijing) Corp, Ltd. was founded in 2012 by Wang Chen and Yao Qicong, both former researchers at WorldQuant, a prominent global quantitative asset management firm. The duo's comprehensive experience in quantitative methods and financial markets laid a strong foundation for Ubiquant, allowing it to steadily evolve within the fast-paced financial landscape.

Initially set up as a commodity trading advisor, Ubiquant carved a niche for itself by employing sophisticated quantitative techniques to optimize trading strategies. In 2014, a key milestone was achieved when Ubiquant registered with the Asset Management Association of China. This registration marked a significant step in their journey, positioning the company to manage investor funds and signal its transition from an advisory role to a fully operational [hedge fund](/wiki/hedge-fund-trading-strategies).

By 2016, Ubiquant had launched its full asset management business. This diversification was fueled by an expanding set of quantitative models and strategies designed to outperform traditional market approaches. As the firm expanded its capabilities, it adapted to the growing complexity and demands of financial markets, leveraging technology and data analytics to bolster its trading prowess. Over this period, Ubiquant cemented its reputation as a significant player in the quantitative hedge fund industry in China, known for its innovative approach and robust investment performance.

Algorithmic and quantitative techniques are integral to shaping these offerings. ## Global Presence and Expansion

Ubiquant Investment (Beijing) Corp, Ltd. has strategically expanded its geographical footprint to strengthen its position in the quantitative hedge fund industry. With offices established in key financial hubs such as Beijing, Shanghai, and Shenzhen, Ubiquant ensures it stays at the forefront of China's rapidly evolving financial market. Notably, in 2021, Ubiquant extended its reach beyond mainland China with the establishment of a subsidiary in Hong Kong. This expansion into Hong Kong, a renowned global financial center, not only broadens Ubiquant's market access but also aligns with its goal of integrating more deeply into the global financial ecosystem.

The strategic choice of positioning its main office near Tsinghua University in Beijing significantly benefits Ubiquant. Tsinghua University, often referred to as the "MIT of China," is renowned for producing top-tier talent in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) fields.

## What is day trading?

Day trading is when people buy and sell stocks or other financial things within the same day. They try to make money from small changes in the prices during that day. Day traders use special tools and charts to help them decide when to buy and sell. They need to watch the market very closely and make quick decisions.

It can be exciting but also risky. Day traders can lose money if the prices move against them. They need a lot of knowledge and experience to be successful. Many people who start day trading lose money because it's hard to predict what will happen in the market. It's important for anyone thinking about day trading to learn a lot and be ready for the risks.

## What is an outside day in trading?

An outside day in trading happens when a stock's price moves beyond both the highest and lowest points of the previous day's trading. Imagine a stock closed at $50 the day before. On an outside day, it might go as low as $48 and as high as $52. This means the new day's price range completely covers the old day's range.

Traders watch for outside days because they can signal that big changes might be coming. If the stock ends the outside day higher than it started, it might mean the price will keep going up. If it ends lower, it could mean the price will keep going down. But, an outside day doesn't always mean a big change will happen. It's just one thing traders look at when deciding what to do next.

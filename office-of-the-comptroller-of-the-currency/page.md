## What is the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC)?

The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) is a part of the U.S. Department of the Treasury. It is in charge of making sure that national banks and federal savings associations follow the rules. The OCC was created in 1863 to make the banking system safer and more stable.

The OCC does its job by checking banks regularly to make sure they are doing things right. They look at how banks handle money, treat customers, and follow laws. If a bank is not following the rules, the OCC can take action to fix the problem. This helps keep the banking system strong and trustworthy.

## When was the OCC established and why?

The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) was established in 1863. This was during the Civil War, and the government wanted to create a stable national currency to help support the war effort and the economy.

The main reason for setting up the OCC was to make the banking system safer and more reliable. Before the OCC, there were many different types of money being used, which made things confusing and risky. By creating the OCC, the government could oversee national banks and make sure they followed the rules, which helped to build trust in the banking system.

## What is the process for chartering a new national bank or federal savings association?

To start a new national bank or federal savings association, you need to go through a process called chartering. First, you have to send an application to the OCC. In the application, you need to give a lot of information about your business plan, who will run the bank, and how much money you have. The OCC looks at this information very closely to make sure you can run a safe and sound bank. They check if your plan makes sense and if the people running the bank have the right skills and experience.

After the OCC reviews your application, they might ask you for more information or to make changes to your plan. If everything looks good, the OCC will give you a preliminary approval. This means you can start getting ready to open your bank. You will need to meet some more requirements, like getting enough money and setting up your systems. Once you have done all of this, the OCC will do a final check. If everything is in order, they will give you a charter, and you can officially open your bank.

## How does the OCC handle enforcement actions and penalties?

When the OCC finds that a bank is not following the rules, they can take steps to fix the problem. They might start by talking to the bank and telling them what they need to change. If the bank does not fix the problem, the OCC can take stronger action. They might make the bank sign a formal agreement to make changes. This agreement is like a promise that the bank will do better.

If the bank still does not follow the rules, the OCC can give them a penalty. This can be a fine, which is money the bank has to pay. The OCC decides how much the fine should be based on how serious the problem is. Sometimes, the OCC might even stop the bank from doing certain things until they fix the problem. By doing these things, the OCC makes sure banks follow the rules and treat customers fairly.

## What are M1 Finance and Wealthfront?

M1 Finance is a company that helps people invest money easily. It combines features of both a robo-advisor and a brokerage account. With M1 Finance, you can create your own investment plan called a "pie," where you choose different stocks and funds to invest in. The platform then automatically manages your investments based on your plan. It's great for people who want some control over their investments but also want the convenience of automatic management.

Wealthfront is another robo-advisor that helps people invest their money. It's designed to be very user-friendly and mostly manages your investments for you. Wealthfront uses a questionnaire to understand your financial goals and risk tolerance, then creates a personalized investment portfolio. It automatically adjusts your investments over time to keep them in line with your goals. Wealthfront is good for people who want a hands-off approach to investing and are looking for a service that does most of the work for them.

## How do M1 Finance and Wealthfront generate revenue?

M1 Finance makes money in a few ways. One main way is through a small fee they charge on certain types of loans called margin loans. When people borrow money from M1 Finance to invest more, they pay interest on that loan. M1 Finance also earns money from payment for order flow, which means they get paid by other companies to send them the orders to buy or sell stocks. Lastly, they make money from interest on cash that customers keep in their accounts.

Wealthfront generates revenue mostly through management fees. They charge a small percentage, usually around 0.25%, of the money that customers have invested with them each year. This fee is for managing the investments and making sure they match the customer's goals. Wealthfront also earns money from interest on cash that customers keep in their accounts, similar to M1 Finance. They might also get some money from payment for order flow, but their main income comes from those management fees.

## What are the minimum investment requirements for M1 Finance and Wealthfront?

M1 Finance does not have a minimum amount of money you need to start investing. You can open an account and begin investing with any amount you want. This makes it easy for anyone to start, even if they don't have a lot of money to invest right away.

Wealthfront also makes it easy to start investing with a low minimum. You only need $500 to open an account and start using their services. This is good for people who have a little more money to invest but still want to keep the starting amount low.

## What types of accounts can be opened with M1 Finance and Wealthfront?

M1 Finance lets you open different types of accounts to help you reach your money goals. You can open a regular investment account, which is called a taxable brokerage account. This is good for people who want to invest money and don't mind paying taxes on what they earn. M1 Finance also offers retirement accounts like a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. These are great if you want to save for when you stop working. If you're saving for a child's education, you can open a custodial account with M1 Finance. They also have a high-yield savings account called M1 Spend, which you can use to save money and spend it easily.

Wealthfront also offers a variety of accounts to help you with your money. You can start with a taxable investment account, which is similar to M1 Finance's brokerage account. This is good for regular investing. Wealthfront also has retirement accounts, like a Traditional IRA and a Roth IRA, to help you save for the future. If you want to save for your kids' education, you can open a 529 college savings plan with Wealthfront. They also offer a cash account, which is like a high-yield savings account where you can keep your money and earn some interest.

## How do the fee structures of M1 Finance and Wealthfront compare?

M1 Finance has a simple fee structure. They don't charge any fees for managing your investments, which is great if you want to keep more of your money. However, if you borrow money from them to invest more, called a margin loan, they charge interest on that loan. The [interest rate](/wiki/interest-rate-trading-strategies) can change, but it's usually around 3% to 8%. M1 Finance also makes money from payment for order flow, which is when they get paid for sending your buy or sell orders to other companies. They also earn a little bit of money from the interest on the cash you keep in your account.

Wealthfront charges a management fee for handling your investments. This fee is 0.25% of the money you have invested with them each year. So, if you have $10,000 invested, you'll pay $25 a year. Wealthfront also makes some money from the interest on the cash you keep in your account, similar to M1 Finance. They might also earn money from payment for order flow, but their main income comes from that 0.25% management fee.

## What investment options are available on M1 Finance and Wealthfront?

M1 Finance lets you invest in a lot of different things. You can pick from over 6,000 stocks and exchange-traded funds (ETFs). They also have pre-made investment plans called "pies" that you can use if you don't want to pick everything yourself. These pies are made to fit different goals, like saving for retirement or growing your money. You can also make your own pie by choosing the stocks and ETFs you like. This way, you can control what you invest in but still have M1 Finance manage it for you.

Wealthfront also gives you many choices for investing. They mainly focus on ETFs, and they have about 200 of them that you can invest in. Wealthfront makes a plan for you based on what you tell them you want and how much risk you're okay with. This plan will include a mix of ETFs that match your goals. They also have special funds like ones that invest in real estate or help the environment. Wealthfront takes care of everything and changes your investments to keep them on track with what you want.

## How do the tax strategies of M1 Finance and Wealthfront differ?

M1 Finance helps you save on taxes with a few different strategies. One way is through tax-loss harvesting, which means they can sell investments that have lost value and use those losses to reduce your taxes. They also let you choose how to handle dividends, like reinvesting them or taking them as cash, which can affect your taxes. M1 Finance gives you control over your investments, so you can make choices that might help you pay less in taxes.

Wealthfront also uses tax-loss harvesting to help you save on taxes. They do this automatically and often, which means they might find more chances to save you money than if you did it yourself. Wealthfront also has a feature called "direct indexing," where they can buy individual stocks to match an index, and this can give them more ways to use tax-loss harvesting. They take care of everything for you, so you don't have to worry about the details, but you still get the tax benefits.

M1 Finance has some cool features for people who know a lot about investing.

## What is an Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)?

An Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) is a type of investment that combines features of mutual funds and stocks. It is a collection of stocks, bonds, or other assets that you can buy and sell on a stock exchange, just like individual stocks. This makes it easy for people to invest in a broad range of assets without having to buy each one separately.

ETFs are popular because they offer diversification, which means they spread out the risk by holding many different investments. They also have lower fees compared to many other types of funds. This makes them a cost-effective way for people to invest in the market and achieve their financial goals.

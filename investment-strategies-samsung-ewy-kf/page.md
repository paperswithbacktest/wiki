## What are EWY and KF, and how do they relate to Samsung?

EWY and KF are two different exchange-traded funds (ETFs). An ETF is like a basket of stocks that you can buy and sell on the stock market. EWY stands for iShares MSCI South Korea ETF, and it includes a bunch of different companies from South Korea. KF stands for Korea Fund, Inc., and it also focuses on South Korean companies. Both of these ETFs give investors a way to invest in South Korea's economy without having to pick individual stocks.

Samsung is a big part of both EWY and KF because it is one of the biggest and most important companies in South Korea. In these ETFs, Samsung's stock makes up a large portion of what's inside the basket. This means that when you buy shares of EWY or KF, you are also investing in Samsung. So, if Samsung does well, it can help the value of these ETFs go up, and if Samsung doesn't do well, it can bring the value down.

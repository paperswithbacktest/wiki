## What is an anticipatory hedge?

An anticipatory hedge is a strategy used by businesses or investors to protect themselves against future price changes. They do this by making a financial move before the change happens. For example, a company might buy a commodity or currency at today's price if they think the price will go up in the future. This way, they can avoid paying more later.

This kind of hedge is common in industries like farming, where prices can change a lot because of things like weather or global demand. By using an anticipatory hedge, a farmer can lock in a price for their crops before they are even harvested. This helps them plan better and reduces the risk of losing money if prices drop suddenly.

## How does anticipatory hedging differ from other types of hedging?

Anticipatory hedging is different from other types of hedging because it involves making a move before a price change happens. For example, if a company thinks the price of a material they need will go up, they might buy it now at the current price. This is different from reactive hedging, where a company waits until the price has already changed before they act. With anticipatory hedging, the goal is to lock in a good price before it goes up, which helps the company avoid paying more later.

Another type of hedging is portfolio hedging, which is used by investors to protect their investments. Portfolio hedging involves buying or selling financial instruments to balance out the risk in an investment portfolio. This is different from anticipatory hedging, which focuses on protecting against future price changes in specific commodities or currencies. While portfolio hedging aims to reduce the overall risk of a set of investments, anticipatory hedging is more about managing the cost of specific goods or services that a company needs.

## What tools and strategies are typically used in anticipatory hedging?

For example, a farmer might use a futures contract to sell their crops at today's price before they are even harvested.

# What is the **Term Spread Trade**?

Term Spread Trade, also known as relative value trade, is a market position where an investor holds a long position in one contract while holding a short position in another contract of the same or similar underlying asset. The spread is the difference between the long and short positions.

Unlike other forms of trading, the aim of spread trading is not to profit from price movements in the underlying asset. Instead, the trader seeks to profit from the widening or narrowing of the spread. Buying a spread means betting on its widening while selling a spread means betting on its narrowing.

# Different forms of spread trading

There are a number of different types of spread trading:

- **Intra-market Calendar Spreads**: These are trades made by buying and selling contracts with different delivery dates. They are commonly used with commodities.
- **Bond Yield Spread Trading**: This is the difference in yield between two bonds of different maturities. The key indicator here is the yield curve.

# The yield curve: a key indicator

The yield curve reflects bond yields for different maturities. Under normal circumstances, a long-term bond presents a higher risk and therefore offers a higher yield. However, depending on economic conditions, this curve may flatten or steepen. For example, in times of economic expansion, the curve tends to steepen, while in times of recession, it tends to flatten.

# Term Spread Trade and algo trading

With the advent of modern technology, Term Spread Trade can be easily integrated with **algo trading**. Algorithms can be programmed to continuously monitor price spreads and execute orders when certain conditions are met. This automation enables greater reactivity and precision, maximizing profit potential.

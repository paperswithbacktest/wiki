## What is Ethereum 2.0?

Ethereum 2.0 is a major upgrade to the Ethereum blockchain, designed to make it faster, more secure, and more energy-efficient. The original Ethereum used a system called "Proof of Work," where computers around the world solve complex math problems to add new transactions to the blockchain. This process uses a lot of electricity and can be slow. Ethereum 2.0 switches to a new system called "Proof of Stake," which chooses validators based on the amount of Ethereum they hold and are willing to "stake" as a security deposit. This change makes the network more energy-efficient and can process transactions much quicker.

The upgrade also introduces something called "sharding," which splits the Ethereum network into smaller pieces called shards. Each shard can process its own transactions and smart contracts, which means the overall network can handle many more transactions at the same time. This is like having multiple lanes on a highway instead of just one, allowing more traffic to flow smoothly. Ethereum 2.0 aims to solve the problems of slow transaction times and high fees that users often face on the current Ethereum network, making it a more scalable and user-friendly platform for decentralized applications and other uses.

## How does Ethereum 2.0 differ from Ethereum 1.0?

Ethereum 2.0 is a big change from Ethereum 1.0. The main difference is how they handle adding new transactions to the blockchain. Ethereum 1.0 uses a system called "Proof of Work," where computers solve hard math problems to add new transactions. This uses a lot of electricity and can be slow. Ethereum 2.0 switches to "Proof of Stake," where people can become validators by holding and locking up their Ethereum as a deposit. This way, it uses much less energy and can add transactions faster.

Another big change is how Ethereum 2.0 makes the network bigger and faster. Ethereum 1.0 has just one chain where all transactions happen, which can get crowded and slow. Ethereum 2.0 uses something called "sharding," which breaks the network into smaller parts called shards. Each shard can handle its own transactions and smart contracts. This means the whole network can handle a lot more transactions at the same time, making everything faster and cheaper for users.

## What is the consensus mechanism change from Ethereum 1.0 to Ethereum 2.0?

Ethereum 1.0 uses a system called Proof of Work for its consensus mechanism. This means miners use powerful computers to solve hard math problems. When they solve a problem, they can add a new group of transactions to the blockchain. This process uses a lot of electricity and can be slow. Miners get paid in Ethereum for their work, but it's not good for the environment because it uses so much power.

Ethereum 2.0 switches to a new system called Proof of Stake. Instead of using computers to solve math problems, people can become validators by locking up, or "staking," their Ethereum as a deposit. The more Ethereum someone stakes, the better chance they have of being chosen to add new transactions to the blockchain. This way, it uses much less energy and can add transactions much faster. It's a big change that makes Ethereum more friendly to the environment and more efficient.

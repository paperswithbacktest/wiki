## What are mutual funds and ETFs?

Mutual funds are investment vehicles that pool money from many investors to buy a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other assets. They are managed by professional fund managers who make decisions about which securities to buy and sell. This makes mutual funds a good option for people who want to invest in the stock market but don't have the time or expertise to manage their own investments. When you invest in a mutual fund, you buy shares of the fund, and the value of your investment rises and falls with the performance of the fund's underlying assets.

ETFs, or Exchange-Traded Funds, are similar to mutual funds in that they also pool investors' money to buy a diversified portfolio of assets. However, ETFs are traded on stock exchanges, just like individual stocks. This means you can buy and sell ETF shares throughout the trading day at market prices, unlike mutual funds which are only priced and traded at the end of the trading day. ETFs often track a specific index, like the S&P 500, and aim to replicate its performance. This makes them a popular choice for investors looking for a low-cost, passive investment strategy.

## What is Procter & Gamble and why is it a popular investment?

Procter & Gamble, often called P&G, is a big company that makes and sells many products people use every day. They make things like shampoo, soap, diapers, and cleaning supplies. People like to invest in P&G because it is a stable and reliable company. It has been around for a long time, over 180 years, and it keeps making money even when the economy is not doing well. P&G also pays dividends, which means it gives some of its profits back to its investors. This makes it attractive for people who want a steady income from their investments. Plus, since everyone needs things like soap and diapers, P&G's products are always in demand.

## How can investors buy shares of Procter & Gamble through mutual funds and ETFs?

Investors can buy shares of Procter & Gamble through mutual funds by choosing funds that include P&G in their portfolio. Many mutual funds focus on large, stable companies like P&G, so you can look for funds that invest in consumer goods or blue-chip stocks. When you invest in these mutual funds, you are indirectly investing in P&G because the fund uses your money to buy shares of the company along with other stocks. This way, you get a piece of P&G without having to buy its shares directly.

ETFs are another way to invest in P&G. You can find ETFs that track indexes which include P&G, like the S&P 500. When you buy shares of these ETFs, you are buying a small part of all the companies in the index, including P&G. ETFs are traded on stock exchanges, so you can buy and sell them easily during the trading day. This makes it simple to get exposure to P&G and other big companies through a single investment.

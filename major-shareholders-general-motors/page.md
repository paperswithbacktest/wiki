## What is a major shareholder?

A major shareholder is someone who owns a big part of a company's stock. This means they have a lot of shares, which gives them a strong say in how the company is run. Usually, a major shareholder is someone who owns at least 5% or more of the company's total shares. They can be a person, a group of people, or even another company.

Major shareholders have a lot of power because they can influence important decisions. For example, they can vote on who should be on the board of directors or whether the company should merge with another company. Because they own so much of the company, what they think and do can really affect the company's future. This is why companies often pay close attention to what their major shareholders want.

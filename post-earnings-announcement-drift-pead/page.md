## What is Post Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD)?

Post Earnings Announcement Drift, or PEAD, is when a company's stock price keeps moving in the same direction after it announces its earnings. If the earnings are better than expected, the stock price might keep going up for a while. If the earnings are worse than expected, the stock price might keep going down.

This happens because not all investors react to the earnings news right away. Some investors might need more time to understand the news or might wait to see if other people think the news is important. This slow reaction can cause the stock price to drift in the direction of the earnings surprise for several weeks or even months after the announcement.

## How was PEAD first identified and studied?

PEAD was first identified and studied in the 1960s by researchers who noticed that stock prices didn't immediately reflect all the information from earnings announcements. One of the earliest studies was done by Ray Ball and Philip Brown in 1968. They looked at how stock prices changed after companies announced their earnings. They found that if a company's earnings were higher than expected, the stock price would keep going up for a while after the announcement. If the earnings were lower than expected, the stock price would keep going down.

Since then, many other researchers have studied PEAD to understand why it happens and how long it lasts. They found that not all investors react to earnings news at the same time. Some investors might need more time to understand the news or might wait to see what other investors do. This slow reaction can cause the stock price to drift in the direction of the earnings surprise for several weeks or even months. Researchers have also looked at how different factors, like the size of the company or the amount of the earnings surprise, can affect how strong the drift is.

## How does market efficiency relate to PEAD?

Market efficiency means that stock prices should quickly reflect all available information. If markets were perfectly efficient, the stock price would change right away after an earnings announcement, and there wouldn't be any drift. But, because of PEAD, we see that stock prices don't always adjust immediately. This shows that markets aren't perfectly efficient, and it takes time for all investors to understand and react to new information.

PEAD happens because not all investors react to earnings news at the same time. Some might need more time to understand the news or might wait to see what other investors do. This slow reaction can cause the stock price to keep moving in the direction of the earnings surprise for weeks or even months. So, PEAD is a sign that markets are not as efficient as they could be, and it gives investors a chance to make money if they can spot stocks that are likely to drift.

## What are the risks associated with trading based on PEAD?

Trading based on PEAD can be risky because the stock market can be unpredictable. Even if a company's earnings are a lot better or worse than expected, the stock price might not keep moving in the direction you expect. Other things, like news about the company or changes in the economy, can affect the stock price and make it go the other way. So, if you buy a stock hoping it will keep going up because of a good earnings surprise, you might lose money if the price goes down instead.

Another risk is that PEAD might not last as long as you think. Sometimes, the stock price might only drift for a short time before it stops or changes direction. If you hold onto the stock for too long, waiting for the price to keep going up or down, you could miss out on making money or even lose money if the price goes the other way. So, it's important to keep an eye on the stock and be ready to sell or buy back quickly if things don't go as planned.

## What advanced statistical methods are used to measure PEAD?

To measure PEAD, researchers often use a statistical method called regression analysis. This helps them see how much a stock's price changes after an earnings announcement. They look at the difference between the actual earnings and what people expected, which is called the earnings surprise. By using regression analysis, they can figure out if the stock price keeps moving in the direction of the surprise and how strong that movement is. This method also helps them see if other things, like the size of the company or how much attention it gets from analysts, affect how much the stock price drifts.

Another method researchers use is event study methodology. This involves looking at how a stock's price changes around the time of the earnings announcement. They compare the stock's returns to what they would expect if there was no earnings surprise. This helps them see if the stock price drifts more than usual after the announcement. Event studies can also show how long the drift lasts and if it's different for different types of companies or sectors. Both regression analysis and event study methodology help researchers understand PEAD better and see how it works in the real world.

## How has the phenomenon of PEAD evolved with changes in market technology and information dissemination?

The way Post Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD) works has changed a lot because of new technology and how information spreads faster now. In the past, it took a long time for people to find out about a company's earnings. They had to wait for newspapers or financial reports to come out. But now, with the internet and social media, earnings news spreads very quickly. This means investors can react to the news faster, which might make PEAD shorter. However, even with faster information, not all investors react at the same time. Some might still need more time to understand the news or might wait to see what others do, so PEAD still happens.

Despite the faster spread of information, PEAD has not gone away completely. It has just changed a bit. For example, some research shows that while the drift might be shorter now, it can still be strong, especially for companies with big earnings surprises. Also, with more data available online, investors can use advanced tools and algorithms to spot stocks that might have PEAD. This means that while technology has changed how PEAD works, it's still an important thing for investors to think about when they're trying to make money in the stock market.

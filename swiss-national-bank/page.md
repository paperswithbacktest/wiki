## What is the Swiss National Bank?

The Swiss National Bank, often called the SNB, is the central bank of Switzerland. It is responsible for managing the country's money and making sure the economy stays stable. The SNB does this by controlling how much money is in circulation and setting interest rates, which are the costs people pay to borrow money.

One of the main jobs of the SNB is to keep inflation low. Inflation is when prices for things like food and clothes go up over time. The SNB tries to keep inflation at around 0 to 2 percent each year. They do this by changing interest rates or buying and selling currencies. This helps to keep the Swiss franc, Switzerland's money, strong and stable.

The SNB also helps the Swiss government with its money matters. It keeps the government's money safe and helps it borrow money when needed. The bank works to make sure that Switzerland's financial system is safe and works well. This helps businesses and people in Switzerland trust the economy and feel confident about their money.

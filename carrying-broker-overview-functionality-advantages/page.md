## What is a carrying broker?

A carrying broker is a type of brokerage firm that holds and manages the securities and cash of its clients. When you invest through a carrying broker, they keep your stocks, bonds, and money safe. They also handle the paperwork and record-keeping needed for your investments. This is different from an introducing broker, which finds clients but doesn't hold their assets.

Carrying brokers are important because they make sure everything is in order and follows the rules set by financial authorities. They have to report to regulators and make sure all transactions are done correctly. This helps protect investors and keeps the market fair and transparent. So, when you use a carrying broker, you can feel more secure knowing your investments are being looked after properly.

## How does a carrying broker differ from an introducing broker?

A carrying broker and an introducing broker play different roles in the world of investments. A carrying broker is the one that actually holds your money and investments, like stocks and bonds. They are responsible for keeping everything safe and making sure all the paperwork and records are correct. They also have to follow the rules set by financial regulators, which helps protect you and keeps the market fair.

On the other hand, an introducing broker doesn't hold your assets. Instead, they focus on finding new clients and helping them start investing. They work with a carrying broker to make sure your investments are managed properly, but they don't handle the actual money or securities. So, while an introducing broker helps you get started, it's the carrying broker that takes care of your investments once you're in the market.

## What are the regulatory requirements for a carrying broker?

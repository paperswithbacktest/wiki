## What are the P5+1 countries?

The P5+1 countries are a group of six nations that work together on important issues, especially about nuclear weapons. The group includes the five permanent members of the United Nations Security Council, which are the United States, Russia, China, France, and the United Kingdom. The "+1" is Germany. These countries are also called the E3+3 because the group includes three European countries (France, Germany, and the UK) and three other major powers (the US, Russia, and China).

The P5+1 group is most known for trying to stop Iran from making nuclear weapons. They worked together to make a deal with Iran called the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), which was signed in 2015. This deal put limits on Iran's nuclear program to make sure they could not build nuclear weapons. The P5+1 countries meet and talk often to make sure the deal is working and to discuss other important world issues.

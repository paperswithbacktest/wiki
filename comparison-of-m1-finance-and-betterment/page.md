## What are M1 Finance and Betterment?

M1 Finance is a platform that helps people invest their money. It lets you create a portfolio of stocks and exchange-traded funds (ETFs) based on what you want to achieve with your money. You can choose from pre-made portfolios or build your own. M1 Finance also offers features like automatic investing and borrowing against your investments. It's designed to be easy to use, even if you're new to investing.

Betterment is another platform that helps people invest and manage their money. It focuses on creating diversified portfolios made up of ETFs. Betterment uses technology to manage your investments automatically, adjusting them to keep your portfolio balanced. It also offers tools to help you plan for retirement and save for other goals. Like M1 Finance, Betterment aims to make investing simple and accessible for everyone.

## How do M1 Finance and Betterment cater to beginner investors?

M1 Finance makes investing easy for beginners by letting them choose from ready-made portfolios or create their own. These portfolios are called "pies," and they can be filled with stocks and ETFs. Beginners can start with expert-made pies that match their goals, like saving for a house or retirement. M1 Finance also has a feature called "auto-invest," which automatically puts money into your pies at set times. This helps beginners invest without having to think about it every day.

Betterment is also great for people new to investing. It creates a diversified portfolio of ETFs for you based on your goals and how much risk you want to take. Betterment does all the work of managing your investments, so you don't have to worry about it. It also has tools that help you plan for the future, like retirement calculators. This makes it easy for beginners to start investing and stay on track with their financial goals.

## What are the fee structures for M1 Finance and Betterment?

M1 Finance has a simple fee structure. If you use their basic service, it's free. You can invest in stocks and ETFs without paying any management fees. But if you want to use their premium service, called M1 Plus, it costs $10 a month or $95 a year. M1 Plus gives you extra features like borrowing against your investments and getting a higher [interest rate](/wiki/interest-rate-trading-strategies) on your cash.

Betterment also has two types of accounts with different fees. Their basic account, called Betterment Digital, charges a low fee of 0.25% per year on the money you invest. This fee is taken out of your account automatically. If you want more help and advice, you can get Betterment Premium, which costs 0.40% per year. Betterment Premium includes unlimited access to financial experts who can help you with your investments and plans.

## How do the investment options differ between M1 Finance and Betterment?

M1 Finance gives you a lot of freedom to pick what you want to invest in. You can choose from a big list of stocks and ETFs. They have ready-made portfolios called "pies" that are made by experts, but you can also make your own pie. This means you can mix and match stocks and ETFs to fit what you want. M1 Finance is good if you want to pick your own investments or if you want to use expert choices but still have control.

Betterment is different because it picks your investments for you. It uses ETFs to make a portfolio that matches your goals and how much risk you want to take. You don't get to choose the specific ETFs; Betterment does it all for you. This makes it easier because you don't have to think about which ETFs to pick. Betterment is good if you want someone else to manage your investments and keep them balanced.

## What are the minimum investment requirements for each platform?

M1 Finance is great for people who don't have a lot of money to start with. You can begin investing with no minimum amount. This means you can start with whatever you have, even if it's just a little bit. It's easy for anyone to get started and begin growing their money.

Betterment also makes it easy to start investing, but it has a small minimum. For their basic account, Betterment Digital, you need at least $10 to start. If you want their premium service, Betterment Premium, you need at least $100,000. This means Betterment might be a bit harder to start with if you don't have much money, but it's still not too high for most people.

## How do M1 Finance and Betterment handle tax strategies like tax-loss harvesting?

M1 Finance does not offer tax-loss harvesting. This means if you use M1 Finance, you won't get help with this tax strategy. You'll need to manage any tax strategies on your own. M1 Finance focuses on making investing easy and letting you pick your own investments, but it doesn't include extra services like tax-loss harvesting.

Betterment, on the other hand, does offer tax-loss harvesting. This means Betterment will help you save on taxes by selling investments that have lost value and buying similar ones. This can lower your tax bill without changing your investment plan much. Betterment uses technology to do this automatically, so you don't have to think about it. This can be a big help if you want to save money on taxes while investing.

## What kind of customer support can users expect from M1 Finance and Betterment?

M1 Finance offers customer support through email and a help center. If you have questions or need help, you can send them an email and they will get back to you. They also have a lot of articles and guides in their help center that can answer common questions. This means you can often find what you need without waiting for an email. But if you need to talk to someone right away, M1 Finance might not be the best choice because they don't offer phone support or live chat.

Betterment provides more ways to get help. You can reach them by email, phone, or live chat. This means if you need quick help, you can call them or use the live chat feature to talk to someone right away. Betterment also has a help center with lots of articles and guides. If you have the Betterment Premium account, you get even more help because you can talk to financial experts who can give you advice on your investments and plans.

## How do the platforms' user interfaces compare in terms of usability and features?

M1 Finance has a user-friendly interface that is easy to navigate, especially for beginners. When you log in, you see your pies, which are your investment portfolios, right away. You can easily add money to your pies or change them by dragging and dropping stocks and ETFs. M1 Finance also has a mobile app that works well and lets you manage your investments on the go. The interface is simple and clean, but it doesn't have as many advanced features as some other platforms. If you want to do things like tax-loss harvesting, you'll need to look elsewhere.

Betterment's interface is also designed to be easy to use, but it focuses more on automation and planning. When you log in, you see your overall portfolio and how it's doing. Betterment does all the work of [picking](/wiki/asset-class-picking) and managing your investments for you, so the interface shows you big-picture things like your goals and progress. It also has tools to help you plan for the future, like retirement calculators. The mobile app is good too, letting you check your investments anytime. Betterment's interface might feel a bit less hands-on than M1 Finance's, but it's great if you want a platform that does most of the work for you.

## What advanced features do M1 Finance and Betterment offer for expert investors?

M1 Finance offers some advanced features that can be useful for expert investors. One of these is the ability to create custom pies, where you can pick exactly which stocks and ETFs you want to invest in. This gives you a lot of control over your investments. Another feature is M1 Borrow, which lets you borrow money against your investments. This can be helpful if you need cash but don't want to sell your stocks. M1 Finance also has M1 Plus, a premium service that gives you perks like a higher interest rate on your cash and the ability to trade during extended hours. These features can help expert investors manage their money in more advanced ways.

Betterment also has advanced features that can appeal to expert investors. One key feature is tax-loss harvesting, which can help you save on taxes by selling losing investments and buying similar ones. This can be a big help if you want to lower your tax bill. Betterment Premium offers access to financial experts who can give you personalized advice on your investments and plans. This can be really useful if you want professional help. Betterment also has tools for advanced planning, like retirement calculators and the ability to set up different types of accounts for different goals. These features can help expert investors make smart decisions and plan for the future.

## How do the performance histories of M1 Finance and Betterment compare?

M1 Finance's performance depends a lot on what you choose to invest in. Since you can pick your own stocks and ETFs or use expert-made pies, how well your investments do can be different from someone else's. M1 Finance doesn't share one overall performance number because everyone's portfolio is different. But if you choose well and keep investing regularly, you can do well over time. It's important to remember that past performance doesn't tell you what will happen in the future, so you need to keep an eye on your investments.

Betterment, on the other hand, gives you a more straightforward performance history because it picks your investments for you. They use ETFs to make a balanced portfolio, and they share how these portfolios have done over time. Betterment's performance can be easier to track because everyone with the same risk level and goals will have similar investments. Like M1 Finance, past performance at Betterment doesn't promise future results, but their automated approach can help you stay on track and make adjustments to keep your portfolio doing well.

## What are the security measures in place for accounts on M1 Finance and Betterment?

M1 Finance takes security seriously to keep your money and information safe. They use strong encryption to protect your data when you log in and do transactions. M1 Finance also follows rules set by the government to keep your money safe, like using something called SIPC coverage. This means if something bad happens to M1 Finance, your investments are protected up to $500,000. They also have a system to watch for anything strange happening in your account, so they can stop fraud before it gets too far.

Betterment also has strong security measures to keep your account safe. They use encryption to protect your data and have systems in place to spot and stop fraud. Betterment follows the same government rules as M1 Finance, including SIPC coverage, to make sure your investments are safe. They also let you use two-[factor](/wiki/factor-investing) authentication, which means you need more than just a password to log in. This adds an extra layer of security to make sure only you can access your account.

## How do M1 Finance and Betterment integrate with other financial tools and services?

M1 Finance works well with other financial tools and services to help you manage your money better. You can connect your M1 Finance account to apps like Mint or Personal Capital, which let you see all your money in one place. This can help you keep track of your spending and savings. M1 Finance also lets you move money easily between your bank and your investment account, so you can keep your money flowing where you need it. If you use their premium service, M1 Plus, you can even link your account to get special deals and discounts on other financial products.

Betterment also connects with other financial tools to make managing your money easier. You can link your Betterment account to budgeting apps like Mint or YNAB to see your whole financial picture. Betterment also works with many banks, so you can move money in and out of your investment account without any trouble. If you have Betterment Premium, you get access to financial experts who can help you plan and use other financial services, like setting up retirement accounts or figuring out the best way to save for your goals.

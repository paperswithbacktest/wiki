## What is an income bond?

An income bond is a type of bond where the issuer promises to pay interest to the bondholder only if they have enough money. This means that if the company doesn't make enough profit, they don't have to pay the interest that year. This makes income bonds riskier than regular bonds, but they can offer higher interest rates to attract investors.

Income bonds are often used by companies that are trying to recover from financial difficulties. By not having to pay interest if they don't have the money, these companies can focus on getting back on their feet. Investors who buy income bonds are usually looking for higher potential returns and are willing to take on more risk.

In the world of online brokerage, E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade stand out for their comprehensive platforms and wide range of offerings. Both brokers offer unique features and capabilities, particularly attracting those interested in algorithmic trading. Algorithmic trading, or algo trading, is the use of computer algorithms to automatically execute trades at optimal prices. Algorithms achieve higher speed and precision than any manual trading ever could, opening up opportunities to enhance trading strategies.

This article focuses on comparing E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade in terms of their algorithmic trading features, aiming to help investors discern which platform best aligns with their trading needs. We will explore aspects such as usability, trade experience, order types, trading technology, and costs, offering a comprehensive guide for traders considering either of these platforms.

By understanding the strengths and weaknesses of each broker, traders can more effectively choose a platform to optimize their trading strategy, ensuring that they are well-equipped to achieve their investment goals.

## Table of Contents

## Platform Usability

Both E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade offer intuitive interfaces that cater to traders with varying levels of experience. E*TRADE provides a suite of platforms, including a web-based interface and a customizable desktop platform known as Power E*TRADE. This desktop platform is particularly noted for its ease of use and adaptability to different trading styles. Furthermore, E*TRADE's mobile application is robust, providing traders with the flexibility to manage their portfolios and conduct trades on-the-go efficiently.

Conversely, TD Ameritrade is recognized extensively for its thinkorswim platform. This platform is celebrated for its sophisticated analytics tools and highly customizable workspace, making it a powerful choice for traders who rely heavily on detailed market analysis. The thinkorswim platform provides an extensive range of features, including advanced charting tools and live market insights, which can aid traders in making informed decisions quickly.

Both E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade support conditional orders, which are essential for executing more complex trading strategies. TD Ameritrade, however, provides a broader range of conditional order types, contributing to its slight advantage in scenarios requiring intricate trading commands.

While both brokers deliver a high level of usability, your preference might hinge on the specific features and layout each platform offers. E*TRADE's straightforward, investor-friendly approach might appeal to those seeking simplicity and robustness, whereas TD Ameritrade's thinkorswim platform could be more attractive to traders looking for powerful analytical tools and a highly adaptable workspace. Understanding these nuances can help in selecting the platform that best aligns with individual trading strategies and preferences.

## Trade Experience

Trade experience on both E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade platforms is robust, providing comprehensive tools for both analysis and trade execution. TD Ameritrade's thinkorswim platform distinguishes itself with its strong [backtesting](/wiki/backtesting) capabilities, enabling traders to simulate trades using historical data before executing them in real-time. This feature is particularly beneficial for those looking to refine their strategies and assess performance without financial risk. Additionally, thinkorswim offers comprehensive options analysis, allowing traders to study different scenarios and their potential impacts on portfolio outcomes.

E*TRADE’s Power E*TRADE platform is praised for its solid analytical tools and customization options. The platform includes advanced charting tools, technical analysis overlays, and real-time market data, providing traders with essential resources to make informed decisions. Power E*TRADE also offers a degree of customization, enabling traders to tailor their settings according to their specific preferences, which can streamline their trading processes and enhance usability.

Both E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade provide two mobile trading applications, accommodating a spectrum of trading needs from basic to advanced. The TD Ameritrade app is recognized for its feature-rich analytics and advanced options trading capabilities, making it a robust choice for traders who require detailed insights and flexible execution tools on the go. In contrast, E*TRADE's mobile solutions also deliver a strong performance, emphasizing user-friendly interfaces and continuous access to significant market insights, suitable for traders who prioritize a seamless mobile experience.

While both platforms excel in providing outstanding trade experiences, TD Ameritrade stands out for traders seeking comprehensive in-depth analysis and backtesting functions. Conversely, E*TRADE is highly regarded for its overall mobile trading experience, catering effectively to traders who favor flexibility and accessibility in their trading applications.

## Order Types and Trading Technology

E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade provide a comprehensive range of order types integral to implementing sophisticated trading strategies. Both platforms accommodate the essential limit and stop orders, allowing traders to specify the price at which they are willing to buy or sell securities. However, TD Ameritrade has a notable advantage with its expanded selection of conditional orders, which include the likes of One Cancels Other (OCO) and One Triggers Another (OTA), among others. These conditional orders allow traders to automate entry and [exit](/wiki/exit-strategy) strategies more efficiently, reducing the need for constant manual intervention.

Moreover, when examining trading technology, both brokers are equipped with advanced tools, yet TD Ameritrade’s thinkorswim platform is particularly distinguished by its capacity to automate trading strategies using custom scripting through its proprietary thinkScript language. This scripting capability permits traders to develop and backtest intricate trading algorithms, thereby enhancing the precision and efficiency of trade execution.

It's important to note, however, that neither E*TRADE nor TD Ameritrade offer fractional shares directly within their standard trading platforms. Fractional shares allow investors to buy a portion of a stock, an attractive option for those looking to diversify with limited capital. Nevertheless, both brokers incorporate fractional share trading within specific programs such as robo-portfolios and Dividend Reinvestment Plans (DRIP).

For investors who prioritize automation and [algorithmic trading](/wiki/algorithmic-trading), TD Ameritrade's offerings may provide a more suitable fit. The thinkorswim platform not only allows for comprehensive automation and detailed technical analysis but also caters to various trading preferences with its extensive range of order types and customization options. This makes it especially appealing for traders looking to harness the power of technology to refine their trading strategies.

## Cost Analysis

Both E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade offer commission-free trading on stocks and ETFs, serving as a significant allure for cost-conscious investors looking to minimize transaction costs. This allows traders to focus more on strategy and less on the fees associated with frequent trading activities.

When examining options trading, TD Ameritrade charges $0.65 per options contract. Conversely, E*TRADE provides a slight discount for high-[volume](/wiki/volume-trading-strategy) traders, reducing the cost to $0.50 per contract. This price differentiation could significantly impact traders with high-frequency options trading strategies, offering substantial cost savings over time with E*TRADE.

Regarding futures and options trading, E*TRADE is generally considered to offer more competitive pricing, making it an attractive option for traders involved heavily in these markets. E*TRADE's pricing for futures and options is designed to appeal to those who engage in large volumes, potentially offering reduced costs through scale efficiencies.

Bond trading fees also present differences between the brokers. E*TRADE typically provides more transparent bond fee schedules, allowing investors to better understand potential costs associated with bond trading activities, contributing to more accurate cost calculations and financial planning over time.

Overall trading costs should be evaluated not only on individual transactions but also in relation to one's trading volume and style. While both platforms provide competitive pricing structures, nuanced differences between the two could influence long-term investment costs. Traders focusing on futures or those participating in high-volume options trading might find E*TRADE's pricing structure to be more favorable, contributing to better cost efficiency in extensive trading scenarios.

## Final Verdict

Choosing between E*TRADE and TD Ameritrade ultimately comes down to personal preference and specific trading needs. Both platforms excel in distinct areas, making them appealing to different types of traders.

TD Ameritrade distinguishes itself with its powerful analytics, backtesting, and automation capabilities, facilitated primarily by its thinkorswim platform. This makes it particularly attractive for traders who are focusing on developing and implementing complex algorithmic trading strategies. The platform is equipped with advanced tools for technical analysis, which are essential for those looking to leverage algorithmic approaches in their trading decisions. Traders who prioritize comprehensive analysis and the ability to automate strategies will likely find TD Ameritrade's offerings advantageous.

On the other hand, E*TRADE is recognized for its investor-friendly pricing and robust mobile trading platforms. It offers a cost-effective solution for active traders, particularly in futures and options markets, with competitive pricing structures that can reduce overall trading costs for high-volume users. E*TRADE's mobile apps are notable for their user-friendly interfaces and broad functionality, making them suitable for traders who require reliable access to trading on the go.

Both brokers provide excellent educational resources and superior customer service, supporting the growth and development of traders at all levels of experience. These resources can be particularly beneficial for novice traders who are just starting to navigate the complexities of financial markets.

While TD Ameritrade holds a slight edge in algorithmic trading features due to its robust technology and diverse order type offerings, E*TRADE's pricing advantages and mobile capabilities present strong alternatives. Ultimately, both platforms are highly competent, catering impressively to diverse trading needs, making either a worthy choice based on individual trading goals.

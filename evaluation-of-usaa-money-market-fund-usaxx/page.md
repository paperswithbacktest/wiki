## What is a money market fund?

A money market fund is a type of mutual fund that invests in very safe, short-term debt like government bonds and high-quality corporate bonds. These funds aim to keep their value stable, usually at $1 per share, and they try to give investors a small amount of interest income.

People often use money market funds as a place to park their cash temporarily because they are considered safer than stocks or long-term bonds. They are a good option for someone who wants to keep their money safe but still earn a little bit of interest, like saving for a short-term goal or holding money while deciding where to invest next.

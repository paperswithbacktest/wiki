When you invest with M1 Finance in 2024, you need to think about taxes. If you make money from your investments, like when you sell a stock for more than you paid for it, you might have to pay capital gains tax. The tax rate depends on how long you held the investment. If you held it for less than a year, it's a short-term capital gain, and you'll pay your regular income tax rate on it. If you held it for more than a year, it's a long-term capital gain, and the tax rate is usually lower. ## How does M1 Finance's margin investing work and what are the risks involved?

M1 Finance's margin investing lets you borrow money to buy more investments than you could with just your own money. You can use this borrowed money to increase your investment pie. When you use margin, you're using your investments as a kind of promise to pay back the loan. M1 Finance will charge you interest on the money you borrow, and you need to keep enough value in your account to cover the loan. If your investments go up in value, you can make more money than if you just used your own money. But if they go down, you could lose more than you started with because you still have to pay back the loan.

The main risk with margin investing is that you could lose more money than you put in. If your investments drop a lot, M1 Finance might ask you to put in more money or sell some of your investments to cover the loan. This is called a margin call. If you can't meet the margin call, M1 Finance can sell your investments, and you might end up owing money even after they're sold. Margin investing can be a good way to grow your money faster, but it's important to understand the risks and make sure you're comfortable with them before you start.

## What is an Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)?

An Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) is a type of investment fund that you can buy and sell on a stock exchange, just like stocks. It's designed to track the performance of a specific index, like the S&P 500, a commodity, bonds, or a basket of assets. This means that when you buy shares of an ETF, you're essentially buying a small piece of all the things that the ETF is tracking.

ETFs are popular because they offer a way to diversify your investments easily. Instead of picking individual stocks or bonds, you can invest in a broad market or a specific sector with just one purchase. They also tend to have lower fees than mutual funds, making them a cost-effective option for many investors. Plus, since they trade like stocks, you can buy and sell them throughout the trading day at market prices.

## What is a contrarian investment strategy?

A contrarian investment strategy means going against what most people are doing in the market. Instead of following the crowd, contrarian investors look for opportunities where others see problems. They buy stocks or other investments when they are unpopular and cheap, betting that they will go up in value later. This approach is based on the idea that the market can be wrong sometimes, and by being patient and going against the trend, you can make good profits.

Contrarian investors often look at things like economic cycles, market sentiment, and company fundamentals to find these opportunities. For example, if everyone is selling a certain stock because of bad news, a contrarian might see it as a chance to buy at a low price. They believe that over time, the market will recognize the true value of the investment. However, this strategy can be risky because it takes a lot of patience and the market might not always turn around as expected.

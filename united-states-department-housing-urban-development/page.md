## What is the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD)?

The United States Department of Housing and Urban Development, or HUD, is a government agency that helps people find and keep good homes. It was created in 1965 to make sure everyone in the U.S. has a safe and affordable place to live. HUD works on many programs that help people buy homes, fix up their homes, and even find places to rent if they can't afford to buy.

HUD also helps communities grow and improve. It gives money to local governments and organizations to build new homes, fix old ones, and make neighborhoods better places to live. This includes things like building parks, fixing roads, and helping homeless people find shelter. By doing these things, HUD tries to make sure that everyone has a chance to live in a nice, safe community.

## When was HUD established and why?

The United States Department of Housing and Urban Development, or HUD, was established on September 9, 1965. It was created during the presidency of Lyndon B. Johnson as part of his Great Society program, which aimed to improve the lives of Americans through various social reforms.

HUD was set up mainly to address the growing need for affordable housing and to improve urban areas. At the time, many people were living in poor conditions, and cities were facing challenges like overcrowding and decay. HUD's goal was to help people find safe and affordable homes and to make neighborhoods better places to live.

## What are some of the major programs administered by HUD?

HUD runs many programs to help people with housing. One big program is called the Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher Program. This program helps low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities pay for rent in private homes or apartments. Another important program is the Public Housing program, where HUD gives money to local housing agencies to build and run affordable housing for low-income families. HUD also has the HOME Investment Partnerships Program, which gives money to states and local governments to build, buy, or fix up affordable homes for low-income people.

Another major program is the Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program. This program gives money to cities and counties to help them improve their communities. They can use the money to fix up old buildings, build new ones, or make other improvements like parks and roads. HUD also runs the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), which helps people buy homes by insuring loans so that lenders are more willing to give money to people who might not otherwise get a loan. These programs all work together to help people find and keep good homes and make their neighborhoods better places to live.

## How does HUD combat housing discrimination and promote fair housing?

HUD works hard to stop housing discrimination and make sure everyone has a fair chance to find a good home.

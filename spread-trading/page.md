# What is spread trading?

Spread trading is a form of trading in which you buy one financial instrument while selling another, usually linked or correlated. The difference between the buying and selling prices of these instruments is known as the spread. This strategy is commonly used in the futures and options markets, although it can also be applied to other financial instruments.

# Spread Trading variations

Spread trading can be classified according to several criteria:

- **Temporal Spread**: This involves taking positions on the same financial instrument with different maturities.
- **Inter-commodity spread**: This concerns related but different financial instruments, such as WTI and Brent in the oil market.
- **Inter-market spread**: This involves trading the same or related instruments on different exchanges.

# Spread trading benefits

Spread trading offers several advantages:

- **Risk reduction**: Since positions are taken simultaneously on correlated instruments, the risk is often lower than when trading a single instrument.
- **Cost efficiency**: Trading costs are often reduced, and real-time data is not always required for success.
- **Flexibility**: This strategy can be applied to a variety of asset classes, although commodities are the most commonly used.

# Examples of Spread Trading

A classic example is the spread between WTI and Brent. Although these two types of oil are similar, their spread has a certain predictability. As a result, traders can profit from temporary price differences between these two products.

Another example is the "Frac Spread" between natural gas and propane. This spread exploits the long-term relationship between these two energy products and the supply and demand dynamics that influence them.

# Spread Trading and Algo Trading

With the evolution of technology, Spread Trading can be easily integrated into **algo trading** strategies. Algorithms can be designed to continuously monitor price spreads and execute orders when certain conditions are met, making the strategy more efficient and reactive.

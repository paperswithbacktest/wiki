## What are qualified dividends?

Qualified dividends are a type of dividend that is taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. They are paid by certain U.S. corporations or qualified foreign corporations. To be considered qualified, the dividends must meet specific criteria set by the IRS, such as the stock being held for a certain period of time.

The tax rate for qualified dividends is the same as the long-term capital gains tax rate, which can be 0%, 15%, or 20%, depending on your income. This is usually lower than the tax rate for ordinary income, which can go up to 37%. Because of this, qualified dividends can help reduce the amount of taxes you pay on your investment income.

For dividends to be considered qualified, you need to hold the stock for a certain amount of time. This is called the holding period. The rule is that you must own the stock for more than 60 days during a 121-day period. This 121-day period starts 60 days before the ex-dividend date, which is the first day the stock trades without the dividend.

If you buy the stock just before the dividend is paid and then sell it right after, the dividend won't be qualified. You need to keep the stock for at least 61 days within that 121-day window.

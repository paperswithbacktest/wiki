## What is a runaway gap in financial markets?

A runaway gap in financial markets is a type of price gap that happens when a stock or other asset's price jumps suddenly and continues to move in the same direction. This gap shows that the market is very excited about the asset, and it usually happens in the middle of a strong trend. It means that the price has moved so quickly that it left a gap on the price chart, with no trading happening at the prices in between.

Runaway gaps are important because they can signal that a trend is likely to keep going. Traders often see these gaps as a sign to keep holding onto their investments or to buy more, expecting the price to keep rising. However, it's also important to be careful because sometimes these gaps can lead to big price swings, and the market might change direction suddenly.

## How does a runaway gap differ from other types of gaps?

A runaway gap is different from other types of gaps mainly because it happens during a strong trend, showing that the trend is likely to keep going. It's like the market is so excited about the stock or asset that the price jumps up quickly, leaving a gap on the chart. This is different from a breakaway gap, which happens at the start of a new trend, signaling that the price is breaking away from a previous range or pattern.

Another type of gap is an exhaustion gap, which happens at the end of a trend. This gap shows that the market might be running out of steam, and the price could soon reverse. Unlike a runaway gap, an exhaustion gap suggests that the trend is about to end, not continue. Lastly, there are common gaps, which can happen anytime and don't really mean much for the trend. They often get filled quickly, meaning the price goes back to where the gap was, while runaway gaps usually don't get filled until much later, if at all.

## What are the common causes of a runaway gap?

A runaway gap often happens because of big news or events that make people want to buy a stock quickly. This could be good earnings reports, new product announcements, or other positive news that makes investors excited. When lots of people want to buy at the same time, the price can jump up fast, leaving a gap on the chart.

Another reason for a runaway gap can be market momentum. If a stock has been going up for a while, more and more people might start to buy it, thinking the price will keep rising. This can create a kind of excitement or "FOMO" (fear of missing out), where people rush to buy the stock, pushing the price up even more and causing a gap.

So, technical analysis tools give traders more information to make better decisions about runaway gaps.

## What are iShares?

iShares are a type of exchange-traded fund (ETF) that is managed by BlackRock, a large investment company. ETFs are like baskets that hold a bunch of different investments, like stocks or bonds, all in one place. When you buy an iShare, you're buying a small piece of this basket. This makes it easier for people to invest in a lot of different things without having to buy each one separately.

People like iShares because they can be bought and sold on a stock exchange, just like regular stocks. This means you can trade them anytime during the trading day, unlike some other types of funds that you can only buy or sell at the end of the day. iShares also tend to have lower fees than other types of funds, which can save you money over time. They are popular with both individual investors and big institutions because they offer a simple way to diversify investments.

They also have funds that mix stocks and bonds, which can help balance risk and reward. For people who care about making a positive impact, iShares has funds that focus on environmental, social, and governance ([ESG](/wiki/esg-investing)) criteria. This means they invest in companies that are good for the planet and society.

## How do iShares ETFs work?

iShares ETFs work by pooling money from many investors to buy a collection of assets, like stocks or bonds. This collection is designed to track the performance of a specific index, like the S&P 500. When you buy shares of an iShares ETF, you're buying a small piece of this collection. The value of your shares goes up or down based on how the assets in the fund perform. iShares ETFs are traded on stock exchanges, so you can buy and sell them anytime during the trading day, just like you would with regular stocks.

The price of an iShares [ETF](/wiki/etf-trading-strategies) is based on the total value of all the assets it holds, which is called the net asset value (NAV). The price you see on the stock exchange might be a little different from the NAV because of supply and demand. But usually, these prices stay pretty close to each other. iShares ETFs are popular because they offer a simple way to invest in a lot of different assets at once, and they often have lower fees than other types of funds. This makes them a good choice for people who want to diversify their investments without spending a lot of money on fees.

## How do iShares ETFs differ from mutual funds?

iShares ETFs and mutual funds are both types of investment funds, but they work a bit differently. One big difference is how you can buy and sell them. iShares ETFs trade on stock exchanges, so you can buy or sell them anytime during the trading day, just like stocks. On the other hand, mutual funds can only be bought or sold at the end of the trading day, based on their net asset value (NAV). This makes iShares ETFs more flexible if you want to make quick changes to your investments.

Another difference is in their fees. iShares ETFs usually have lower fees than mutual funds. This means you get to keep more of your money over time, which can make a big difference in the long run. Also, iShares ETFs are designed to track the performance of specific indexes, like the S&P 500, while mutual funds can be actively managed by a fund manager who tries to beat the market. This means iShares ETFs can be a simpler and often cheaper way to invest in a broad range of assets.

## What are the costs associated with iShares ETFs?

When you invest in iShares ETFs, you need to think about the costs. One main cost is the expense ratio, which is a yearly fee that the fund charges to cover its operating costs. This fee is a percentage of the money you have invested in the ETF. iShares ETFs usually have lower expense ratios than many other types of funds, which means you get to keep more of your money. But even small fees can add up over time, so it's good to check the expense ratio before you invest.

Another cost to think about is the trading fee, which is what your broker charges you to buy or sell the ETF. This fee can be different depending on which broker you use. It's a good idea to look at these costs and see how they might affect your investment over time.

## What are the risks involved in investing in iShares ETFs?

Investing in iShares ETFs comes with some risks. One big risk is that the value of your investment can go up or down. This happens because the ETFs hold stocks or bonds, and the prices of those can change a lot. If the market goes down, the value of your ETF might go down too. Another risk is that even though ETFs are designed to track an index, they might not do it perfectly. This can happen because of fees or other costs, or because the ETF might not hold all the same stocks or bonds as the index it's trying to follow.

Another thing to think about is that some ETFs focus on specific countries or industries. If you invest in an ETF like this, your investment could be more risky because it's not spread out as much. For example, if you buy an ETF that only invests in tech companies and the tech industry has a bad year, your ETF could lose value. Also, even though ETFs usually have lower fees than other types of funds, those fees can still add up over time and eat into your returns. It's important to understand these risks and think about how they fit with your investment goals.

## What advanced strategies can be used with iShares ETFs?

One advanced strategy you can use with iShares ETFs is called sector rotation. This means you move your money between different industries based on how the economy is doing. For example, if you think technology companies will do well in the next few months, you might invest more in an iShares ETF that focuses on tech, like the iShares Global Tech ETF. If you think the economy might slow down, you could shift some of your money into an ETF that invests in more stable industries, like utilities or consumer staples. This can help you take advantage of trends in the market and possibly earn more money.

Another strategy is called hedging. This is when you use one investment to protect another. For example, if you own a lot of stocks and you're worried the market might go down, you could buy an iShares ETF that invests in bonds, like the iShares Core U.S. Aggregate Bond ETF. Bonds often go up in value when stocks go down, so this can help balance out your losses. You could also use inverse ETFs, which are designed to go up when the market goes down. For instance, the iShares Short Treasury Bond ETF can be a safe place to park your money if you think the market will be volatile. These strategies can help you manage risk and protect your investments.

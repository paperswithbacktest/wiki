## What is the Kazakhstan Tenge?

The Kazakhstan Tenge is the official money used in Kazakhstan. It started being used on November 15, 1993. Before the Tenge, Kazakhstan used the Russian Ruble. The Tenge helped Kazakhstan become more independent in its money matters.

The Tenge has coins and banknotes. The coins come in values like 1, 2, 5, 10, 20, and 50 Tenge. The banknotes are in amounts like 200, 500, 1000, 2000, 5000, and 10000 Tenge. The money shows pictures that are important to Kazakh culture and history.

The word "Tenge" comes from an old Turkic word meaning "scale" or "balance." This shows how important fairness in trade is to the people of Kazakhstan. The Tenge is managed by the National Bank of Kazakhstan, which works to keep the country's money stable and strong.

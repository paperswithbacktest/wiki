## What is Regulation G?

Regulation G is a rule set by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). It tells public companies how they should share information about their earnings. The main point of Regulation G is to make sure that when companies talk about their earnings, they use clear and honest numbers. This helps investors understand the company's financial health better.

The rule says that if a company wants to talk about a special way of calculating earnings, like "adjusted EBITDA," they must also show the earnings calculated the usual way, called GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles). This way, investors can compare the numbers and see if the special calculation gives a different picture of the company's performance. Regulation G helps keep the information fair and useful for everyone.

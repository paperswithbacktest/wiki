## What is SEC Form 15-12B?

SEC Form 15-12B is a document that companies use to tell the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) that they no longer want to be a public company. When a company files this form, it means they want to stop following the rules that public companies have to follow, like reporting their financial information regularly. This process is called "deregistration" or "going dark."

Once a company files Form 15-12B, it starts a process where the company's stocks will no longer be traded on a big stock exchange like the New York Stock Exchange or NASDAQ. Instead, the company might move to a smaller market or stop trading altogether. This can happen for many reasons, like wanting to save money on reporting costs or because the company is being bought by another company.

## What is a Bump-Up Certificate of Deposit (CD)?

A Bump-Up Certificate of Deposit, or Bump-Up CD, is a special type of savings account offered by banks. It allows you to increase the interest rate on your CD one or more times during its term if the bank's rates go up. This can be helpful if you think interest rates might rise after you open your CD.

When you open a Bump-Up CD, you agree to keep your money in the account for a set period, just like with a regular CD. The difference is that if interest rates go up, you can ask the bank to bump up your rate to the new, higher one. This gives you a chance to earn more money on your savings without having to open a new CD. However, not all banks offer Bump-Up CDs, and the rules can vary, so it's important to check the details before you decide to open one.

## How does a Bump-Up CD differ from a traditional CD?

A Bump-Up CD and a traditional CD are both types of savings accounts where you agree to keep your money in the bank for a certain time. With a traditional CD, you get a fixed interest rate that stays the same until the CD matures. This means you know exactly how much money you will earn, but if interest rates go up, you won't benefit from the higher rates.

A Bump-Up CD, on the other hand, lets you increase your [interest rate](/wiki/interest-rate-trading-strategies) if the bank's rates go up during your CD term. This can be helpful if you think rates might rise after you open your CD. You can ask the bank to bump up your rate to the new, higher one, which could help you earn more money on your savings. However, Bump-Up CDs might start with a lower interest rate than traditional CDs, and you can usually only bump up the rate a limited number of times.

## How do Bump-Up CDs compare to other investment options like bonds or savings accounts?

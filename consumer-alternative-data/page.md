## What is consumer alternative data?

Consumer alternative data refers to information that is not typically found in traditional financial reports or market research. This type of data can come from many different sources, like social media activity, satellite images, or credit card transactions. Companies use this data to learn more about what consumers are doing and thinking, which can help them make better business decisions.

For example, a company might look at social media posts to see what people are saying about their products. This can give them a better idea of what customers like or don't like. Another example is using satellite images to see how busy a store's parking lot is, which can help predict sales. By using this kind of data, businesses can get a more complete picture of the market and stay ahead of their competition.

## How is consumer alternative data different from traditional data?

Consumer alternative data is different from traditional data because it comes from new and unusual sources. Traditional data usually includes things like sales numbers, financial reports, and market research surveys. These are the kinds of data that businesses have been using for a long time to understand their markets and make decisions. On the other hand, alternative data can come from places like social media, mobile apps, satellite images, and even credit card transactions. This type of data gives businesses a fresh look at what consumers are doing and thinking.

The main difference is that [alternative data](/wiki/best-alternative-data) often gives real-time insights into consumer behavior. For example, traditional data might tell a company how many products they sold last quarter, but alternative data could show them how many people are talking about their product on social media right now. This can help businesses react more quickly to changes in the market. Also, alternative data can be more detailed and specific, allowing companies to see trends and patterns that they might miss with just traditional data.

## What are the common sources of consumer alternative data?

Consumer alternative data comes from many different places that are not the usual sources of information. Some common sources are social media platforms like Twitter and Instagram. Companies can look at what people are posting and talking about online to understand what they like or don't like. Another source is mobile apps, which can track how people use their phones and what they do on them. For example, a fitness app might show how often people are exercising, which can tell companies about health trends.

Other sources include satellite images, which can show how busy a store's parking lot is or how many ships are at a port. This helps businesses predict how much they might sell. Credit card transactions are also a big source of alternative data. By looking at what people are buying, companies can see what products are popular and where people are spending their money. Web scraping is another way to get alternative data, where information is collected from websites to see what people are searching for or reading about.

These sources give businesses a lot of new information that they can use to make better decisions. For example, if a company sees that a lot of people are talking about a new type of food on social media, they might decide to start selling it in their stores. Or if satellite images show that a shopping center is getting more visitors, a business might want to open a new store there. This kind of data helps companies stay up to date with what consumers want and need.

## How can consumer alternative data be integrated with existing data systems?

This means they have to change the format of the alternative data so it matches the format of their existing data. Once the data is in the right format, businesses can use special software to bring it all together. This software can help them see patterns and trends by mixing the new alternative data with their old data. For example, a company might look at both their sales numbers and social media posts to understand why sales went up or down. By doing this, they can get a better picture of what is happening in their market and make smarter decisions. But it takes time and special skills to make sure everything works together smoothly.

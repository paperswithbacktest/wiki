## What is a registered bond?

A registered bond is a type of bond where the owner's name is recorded by the issuer. This means that when the bond is issued, the company or government that issued it keeps a record of who owns it. This makes it easier to pay interest and return the money when the bond matures. If the bond owner wants to sell the bond, they need to tell the issuer so the ownership record can be updated.

Registered bonds are different from bearer bonds, where the bond is owned by whoever holds the physical certificate. With registered bonds, even if someone else gets the certificate, they can't claim the bond's payments without changing the ownership record. This makes registered bonds safer and more convenient for both the issuer and the owner.

## How does a registered bond differ from a bearer bond?

A registered bond and a bearer bond are two types of bonds, but they work differently. A registered bond has the owner's name recorded by the issuer. This means the company or government knows who owns the bond and can send interest payments directly to that person. If the owner wants to sell the bond, they need to tell the issuer to change the ownership record.

On the other hand, a bearer bond does not have the owner's name recorded. Instead, whoever holds the physical certificate owns the bond. This means that if you lose the certificate, anyone who finds it can claim the bond's payments. Bearer bonds are less common now because they can be riskier, but they are easier to transfer without any records.

In summary, registered bonds are safer and more convenient because they keep track of ownership, while bearer bonds are anonymous and transferable by just handing over the certificate.

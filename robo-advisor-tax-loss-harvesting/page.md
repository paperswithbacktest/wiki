## What is a robo-advisor?

A robo-advisor is a type of financial service that uses computer algorithms to manage your investments. It's like having a robot help you with your money. You tell the robo-advisor what your goals are, how much risk you're willing to take, and how long you want to invest for. Then, the robo-advisor picks a mix of investments, like stocks and bonds, that fit your needs.

Robo-advisors are popular because they are usually cheaper than hiring a human financial advisor. They often charge a small percentage of the money you invest with them, and they don't need as much money to start as some traditional advisors. This makes them a good choice for people who are just starting to invest or who don't have a lot of money to invest yet.

## What is tax-loss harvesting?

Tax-loss harvesting is a way to save money on taxes when you invest. It happens when you sell an investment that has lost value. Instead of just being sad about losing money, you can use that loss to lower the taxes you pay on other investments that made money. It's like finding a silver lining in a bad situation.

When you do tax-loss harvesting, you sell the investment that lost value and then buy a similar one to keep your investment plan the same. This way, you don't change your overall investment strategy, but you get to use the loss to reduce your taxes. It's a smart move that many people use to make the best of their investments, even when some of them don't do well.

## Are there any risks or limitations to tax-loss harvesting through robo-advisors?

Using a robo-advisor for tax-loss harvesting is great, but there are some things to watch out for. One big thing is that there are rules about how you can use tax losses. For example, if you sell an investment at a loss and then buy the same or a very similar one within 30 days, you can't use that loss to lower your taxes. This is called the wash-sale rule, and robo-advisors have to be careful not to break it. If they do, you might not get the tax savings you were hoping for.

Another thing to think about is that robo-advisors might not be able to see all your investments. If you have money in different places, like different banks or investment accounts, the robo-advisor might only know about the investments it manages. This means it might miss chances to do tax-loss harvesting with all your money. Also, while robo-advisors are good at following rules and making quick decisions, they might not be as good as a human at understanding your whole financial situation and making the best choices for you.

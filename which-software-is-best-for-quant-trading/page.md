Choosing the best software for quantitative trading depends on a number of factors, including the user's specific needs, programming skill level, target markets, and available resources. Here are some of the most reputable software and platforms in the field of quantitative trading:

1. **QuantConnect**:
    - **Description**: A cloud-based platform that supports several programming languages, including C# and Python.
    - **Benefits**: Access to a wide variety of historical data, backtesting and live trading capabilities.
2. **Backtrader** :
    - **Description**: A flexible and versatile Python library for backtesting and trading.
    - **Benefits**: Ability to integrate various data sources, support for multi-timeframe strategies, rich visualization.
3. **MetaTrader 4 & 5 (MT4/MT5)** :
    - **Description**: Mainly focused on Forex, but MT5 also offers other markets such as equities.
    - **Benefits**: Large community, numerous plugins and indicators, integrated environment for developing Expert Advisors (automated strategies).
4. **NinjaTrader** :
    - **Description**: Platform focused on automated trading and backtesting.
    - **Benefits**: Includes advanced tools for technical analysis, support for custom strategy development in NinjaScript.
5. **Zipline** :
    - **Description**: Python library designed for event-driven backtesting, originally used by Quantopian.
    - **Benefits**: Well-suited to quantitative research, extensible with plugins.
6. **Quantlib** :
    - **Description**: Open-source library mainly written in C++, designed for quantitative modeling in finance.
    - **Benefits**: Rich functionality for modeling, risk assessment and trading.
7. **AlgoTrader** :
    - **Description**: Integrated platform for backtesting and automated trading.
    - **Benefits**: Features for machine learning-based strategies, easy integration with various data sources.

If you're new to quantitative trading and looking for a turnkey solution, **MetaTrader** or **NinjaTrader** may be good choices. If you have Python programming skills, **Backtrader** or **Zipline** are excellent options. For a cloud-based solution with maximum flexibility, **QuantConnect** is highly recommended.

Remember, regardless of the software or platform you choose, it's essential to understand the fundamentals of trading, test your strategies carefully and be aware of the risks associated with trading the financial markets.

## What is a Condor Spread Strategy?

A Condor Spread Strategy is a type of options trading strategy that involves buying and selling four different options at the same time. It's called a "condor" because the payoff diagram looks a bit like a condor with its wings spread out. The goal is to make money when the price of the stock stays within a certain range. To set up a condor spread, you buy one call option with a higher strike price, sell another call option with a slightly lower strike price, sell a put option with a slightly higher strike price than the second call, and buy a put option with an even lower strike price.

This strategy is often used when someone thinks the stock price won't move too much in either direction. If the stock price stays between the middle two strike prices at expiration, all the options will expire worthless, and the trader keeps the premium they collected from selling the options. However, if the stock price moves outside this range, the trader could lose money. The maximum profit is limited to the net premium received, while the maximum loss is also limited, making it a defined risk strategy. It's a good choice for traders who want to make a bit of money with a known risk if things don't go as planned.

## What are On-The-Run Treasuries?

On-The-Run Treasuries are the most recently issued U.S. Treasury securities. They are called "on-the-run" because they are the latest ones available and are actively traded in the market. These securities can be Treasury bills, notes, or bonds, and they are considered the most liquid and up-to-date options for investors looking to buy government debt.

When a new Treasury security is issued, it becomes the new on-the-run security, and the previous one becomes an off-the-run security. This means that on-the-run Treasuries usually have a higher trading volume and narrower bid-ask spreads compared to off-the-run Treasuries. Investors often prefer on-the-run Treasuries because they can be easily bought and sold, making them a popular choice for those who need to quickly adjust their investment portfolios.

## How do On-The-Run Treasuries differ from Off-The-Run Treasuries?

This makes them a good choice for people who want to quickly change their investments.

Off-The-Run Treasuries are older U.S. Treasury securities that were issued before the latest ones. They are called "off-the-run" because they are not the newest and do not get traded as much. These securities are less liquid, meaning it can be harder to buy or sell them quickly. They usually have lower trading volumes and wider bid-ask spreads, which can make them less attractive to some investors who need flexibility in their investments.

## How are On-The-Run Treasuries issued by the U.S. Treasury?

On-The-Run Treasuries are issued by the U.S. Treasury through a process called an auction. ## What are the typical maturities for On-The-Run Treasuries?

On-The-Run Treasuries come in different types, each with its own typical maturity. Treasury bills, or T-bills, are short-term securities that usually mature in a few weeks to one year. They are used by the government to meet short-term financial needs. Treasury notes, or T-notes, have longer maturities, typically ranging from two to ten years. These are popular among investors looking for a bit more stability and a steady income over a longer period.

Treasury bonds, or T-bonds, are the longest-term On-The-Run Treasuries, with maturities of 20 or 30 years. They are used by investors who want to lock in interest rates for a long time and are looking for a safe investment over many years. Each type of On-The-Run Treasury serves different needs in the market, from short-term liquidity to long-term investment stability.

## How do yield curves influence the trading strategies for On-The-Run Treasuries?

Yield curves show how the interest rates of different On-The-Run Treasuries change over time. They help investors see if short-term rates are higher or lower than long-term rates. When the yield curve is normal, it goes up, meaning long-term rates are higher than short-term rates. This can make investors want to buy long-term On-The-Run Treasuries to get higher returns. But if the yield curve is flat or even goes down, it might make investors think twice about buying long-term securities because they might not get much more return for waiting longer.

Investors use the shape of the yield curve to decide their trading strategies. If they think the yield curve will keep going up, they might buy more long-term On-The-Run Treasuries to lock in those higher rates. But if they think the yield curve might flatten or go down, they might stick to short-term securities or even sell their long-term ones to avoid losing money. The yield curve is a big deal because it helps investors guess what might happen with interest rates and plan their moves in the market.

## What are the risks associated with trading On-The-Run Treasuries?

These risks are important to think about when trading On-The-Run Treasuries.

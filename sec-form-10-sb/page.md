## What is SEC Form 10-SB?

SEC Form 10-SB is a document that small businesses use to register their stocks with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). It is designed for companies that are considered "small business issuers," which means they have less than $25 million in annual revenues and no public float, or the value of their publicly traded shares is less than $25 million. This form helps these smaller companies to comply with federal securities laws without the burden of the more detailed reporting requirements that larger companies face.

Using Form 10-SB, a small business can provide important information about its operations, financial condition, and management to potential investors. This includes details like the company's business model, financial statements, and any risks involved in investing in the company. By registering with the SEC through this form, the company can legally offer its stocks to the public, which can help it raise the capital needed to grow and expand.

## What are the deadlines for filing SEC Form 10-SB?

When a small business wants to file SEC Form 10-SB, it needs to do it within a certain time. The company has to file the form within 90 days after the end of its fiscal year. This means if the company's fiscal year ends on December 31, it needs to file the form by March 31 of the next year.

If the company misses this deadline, it might have to pay a fine or face other problems with the SEC. It's important for the company to keep track of this date and make sure all the information is ready on time. By filing on time, the company can stay in good standing with the SEC and continue to offer its stocks to the public.

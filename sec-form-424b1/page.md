## What is SEC Form 424B1?

SEC Form 424B1 is a document that companies file with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). It is used when a company wants to sell new stocks or bonds to the public. This form gives important details about the securities being offered, like the price, how many are being sold, and any risks involved. It helps investors make smart choices by giving them all the information they need.

The form is part of the registration statement that companies must file before they can sell securities. It is usually filed after the initial registration statement, which is called Form S-1. Form 424B1 includes the final details of the offering, which might have changed since the initial filing. This makes sure that the information investors see is up-to-date and accurate.

        risk_evaluation = "High Risk" if "High" in row['Risk Factors'] else "Moderate Risk"

        print(f"Company: {row['Company']}")

        print("--------")

assess_investment(data)

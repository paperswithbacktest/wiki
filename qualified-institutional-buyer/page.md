## What is a Qualified Institutional Buyer (QIB)?

A Qualified Institutional Buyer (QIB) is a type of investor that is considered to be sophisticated and financially stable. These investors are usually large institutions like banks, insurance companies, and pension funds. They are allowed to buy certain securities that are not available to the general public, such as private placements and certain types of bonds. The reason they can do this is because they have a lot of money and are thought to understand the risks involved.

The main rule that defines a QIB is set by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). According to the SEC, to be a QIB, an institution must own and invest at least $100 million in securities. This high threshold ensures that only big and experienced investors can be QIBs. By limiting these investments to QIBs, the government hopes to protect smaller investors from risky deals that they might not fully understand.

## What is the CFP Franc?

The CFP Franc is a currency used in several French Pacific territories. These territories include French Polynesia, New Caledonia, and Wallis and Futuna. The letters "CFP" stand for "Change Franc Pacifique," which means "Pacific Franc Exchange" in English. The currency is also sometimes called the "Pacific Franc."

The CFP Franc is linked to the Euro. This means that the value of the CFP Franc stays the same compared to the Euro. For every 1 Euro, you get 119.33 CFP Francs. This link makes it easier for these French territories to trade with Europe. The French government manages the CFP Franc to keep its value stable.

This link helped the territories trade with France and other places more easily.

Over the years, the CFP Franc changed a bit. In 1985, it was linked to the French Franc at a fixed rate. This meant that the value of the CFP Franc stayed the same compared to the French Franc. When the Euro became France's money in 1999, the CFP Franc was then linked to the Euro. Now, for every 1 Euro, you get 119.33 CFP Francs.
